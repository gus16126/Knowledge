Transporting Specialty Vehicles

Specialty vehicles are any vehicles that fall outside the normal size range, including modified vehicles or unmodified larger vehicles. 

The following instructions will guide you through the process of moving and handling specialty vehicles, emphasizing the importance of extra caution due to their unique clearances and step-in heights.

- Extra Caution- Use extra caution when moving around and getting in and out of specialty vehicles, as the normal clearances and step-in heights can be drastically different from those of normal vehicles.

**==Wrong Training Instructions.==**
- **Loading Tall, Heavy, or Wide Vehicles - If moving especially tall, heavy, or wide vehicles (e.g., cargo vans, dually trucks), they will be the only vehicle loaded onto the truck. When moving a single vehicle, ensure it is fully pulled forward onto the wheel stopper behind the cab.**
**==Cottrell has very specific instruction and diagram for loading Tall, Heavy, or Wide Vehicles that strongly contradict these instructions.==**

- Locating Tires - Locate the front tires as you would a normal pick-up truck and ensure the inside rear wheels are located as normal single rear tires of a pick-up truck would be.
- Vehicle Weights - Most vehicle weights can be found in the Black Book Invoicing Guides. These are available in the purchasing office or online at [https://www.blackbookusa.com](https://www.blackbookusa.com/).
- Verifying Height and Clearances - Always verify the height before transportation and know the clearances along your route. Read the clearance signs before crossing under any unfamiliar bridges, underpasses, or other restricted height obstructions.
- Determining Newer Vehicle Weights - Newer vehicle weights can be determined by using the

GWR weight on the VIN tag and subtracting the cargo and occupant weight found on the TIRE AND LOADING INFORMATION tag located on the driver's door jamb.

Always prioritize using extra caution due to their unique clearances and step-in heights. 

**==Wrong Training Instructions.
**Ensure that tall, heavy, or wide vehicles are loaded individually and fully pulled forward onto the wheel stopper.**
