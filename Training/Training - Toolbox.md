🟦 Team Training – Toolbox Organization & Equipment Readiness

Team,

Today I would like to highlight Truck 232557 as an example of the importance of maintaining our equipment and work areas.

Our new team member, Leonardo, cleaned and reorganized the toolbox, replaced the missing spill kit, added two spare straps with ratchets, and replaced all tie-down straps on both the upper and lower decks. He has also identified three missing wheel chocks and will replace them once they arrive.

This effort demonstrates an important principle: a toolbox is more than a storage compartment—it is a safety and operational resource. A clean, organized, and properly stocked toolbox allows drivers to quickly locate the equipment they need, complete inspections efficiently, and respond appropriately to situations on the road.

Key Expectations:
• Keep toolboxes clean and organized.
• Return equipment to its designated location after use.
• Report missing or damaged items promptly.
• Verify required equipment is present during inspections.
• Help maintain the truck in a condition that sets the next driver up for success.

When we take ownership of our equipment, we support safe operations, improve efficiency, and demonstrate respect for our teammates. Small actions such as replacing missing supplies, organizing equipment, and maintaining readiness contribute directly to our ability to execute with excellence.

Thank you, Leonardo, for your initiative and commitment.
