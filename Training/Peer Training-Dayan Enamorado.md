🟦 Peer Training – Dayan Enamorado

Training Period: May 11 – May 25, 2026

Dayan Enamorado was trained using the refined home delivery onboarding and equipment training model. Although Dayan was already a highly experienced commercial truck driver, he had no prior experience in car hauling. His training focused on the specialized skills required to operate the 2-car hauler equipment safely and efficiently.

Training Model Continuity

Dayan received structured, hands-on instruction focusing on car-hauling specific operations. This included:

- **Equipment Safety & Hydraulics:** Intensive training on the 2-car hauler's hydraulic ramp operations, deck pinning, and height clearance limits.
- **Securement Protocols:** Hands-on practice with the Cottrell QuiXspinz ratcheting system, ensuring proper strap alignment and tensioning to prevent wear and damage.
- **Vehicle Loading & Alignment:** Repetitive practice sessions for safe loading, unloading, and centering of different vehicle classes (including oversized SUVs and sedans).
- **Inspections & Compliance:** Daily Pre-Trip and Post-Trip inspections tailored to the unique mechanical checkpoints of car-hauling trailers, alongside My20 ELD usage and DOT requirements.
- **ELDT Curriculum Alignment:** Although conducted without a formal classroom curriculum, my peer-training model was designed to mirror the structural requirements of the Federal FMCSA Class B CDL Entry-Level Driver Training (ELDT) curriculum. With the exception of select tractor-trailer components (such as trailer coupling/uncoupling) which do not apply to straight trucks, my hands-on instruction systematically covered all key ELDT areas: basic operation controls, safe driving procedures, visual search habits, space/space management, and range-backing maneuvers (including straight line, alley dock, and parallel docking). Aligning peer training with these federal standards ensures our home delivery drivers operate with commercial-grade safety and compliance.

Key Training Focus Areas

- Safe transition to a specialized 2-car hauler straight truck, focusing on loading configurations and maneuvering.
- QuiXspinz strap securement system alignment, tensioning, and wear prevention.
- Hydraulic ramp positioning, deck pinning safety, and vehicle clearance checks.
- Pre-Trip Inspection discipline, identifying car-hauler specific trailer components.
- Maintaining customer service excellence and professional communication during deliveries.

✅ Performance & Engagement

Dayan leveraged his extensive professional truck driving background to master road safety, spatial awareness, and DOT compliance quickly. He showed great focus and dedication during hands-on sessions, adapting rapidly to the unique demands of loading and securing vehicles on the hauler. By the end of the training period, Dayan demonstrated full independence and proficiency in all aspects of 2-car hauler operations and was ready for the road test.

💡 Leadership Coaching & Value Alignment

Dayan was hired to operate out of Store 7113 under Logistics Manager **Ana Lezcano**. During our training conversations and field exercises, I observed four critical development opportunities regarding his professional boundaries, communication style, and alignment with CarMax values:

1. **Constructive Relationship with Leadership:** Dayan initially made comments criticizing his future manager's logistics knowledge and capabilities. Recognizing this behavior as an operational red flag that could undermine team cohesion, I immediately stepped in to address it. Drawing on the professional, empathetic correction framework modeled by my manager, **Matt Douglas**, during my own development discussions, I refocused Dayan on CarMax’s core values of **Win Together** and **Put People First**. I explained that a successful logistics operation relies on mutual trust, open collaboration, and active partnership with leadership. I coached him to approach his transition with respect and a supportive attitude.
2. **Professional Boundaries & Code of Conduct:** Additionally, Dayan expressed assumptions regarding his future manager's personal intentions, misinterpreting her open communication in a way that led to unprofessional boundary expectations. I immediately addressed this behavior, stressing the **CarMax Code of Conduct** and our strict commitment to maintaining a respectful, professional, and high-integrity workplace. Recognizing that this required deeper coaching to ensure full compliance before his transfer, I partnered with my manager, **Matt Douglas**, to request additional training hours. Without disclosing the sensitive details of the conversation, I secured the necessary time to conduct dedicated development sessions focusing on professional workplace conduct and boundaries.
3. **Brand Representation & Professional Vocabulary:** During a MaxOffer pickup at a partner dealership, Dayan engaged in a loud and overly informal conversation with a dealer employee, utilizing improper vocabulary that drew negative attention from the dealership's management. Immediately following the visit, I addressed the situation, explaining how such behavior impacts CarMax's reputation and partner relationships. We reviewed the **CarMax Code of Conduct** and our core value of **Do the Right Thing** regarding professional communication, establishing that professional vocabulary is required in all public-facing and peer-to-peer environments to safeguard CarMax's brand image.
4. **Workplace Respect & Public Representation:** While walking through the CarMax open appraisal lot, Dayan made unprofessional and inappropriate verbal remarks regarding a female customer's appearance. I immediately de-escalated the situation in public by maintaining movement to avoid drawing attention. Once inside the truck, I conducted direct, one-on-one coaching to address the behavior. I reviewed the **CarMax Code of Conduct** and our **Do the Right Thing** core value, emphasizing that treating everyone—including customers and the public—with absolute respect is non-negotiable. I explained that while in uniform and on duty, our actions directly project onto CarMax's public image, and that comments overheard in public spaces present a severe brand reputation risk. Dayan was highly receptive to the feedback, acknowledging that he had noticed my immediate non-verbal boundary setting. He fully accepted the correction and committed to upholding these professional standards.

Through these structured coaching sessions and subsequent follow-ups, Dayan developed a clear understanding of the professional standards expected of a CarMax associate. While Dayan successfully completed his equipment training, he ultimately chose not to continue his employment with CarMax.

**Scope of Documentation Note:**
Under normal circumstances and in alignment with our commitment to **Put People First**, coaching conversations of this nature are handled privately at the trainer-trainee level to protect the associate's professional integration and prevent unnecessary bias. However, because Dayan did not continue his employment with the company, and because these coaching sessions represent critical milestones in my own Individual Development Plan (IDP) focusing on **Analysis & Decision Making**, these details are included here to demonstrate my active leadership growth, values alignment, and peer coaching capabilities.

**Managerial Resolution Strategy:**
My decision to address these coaching opportunities directly rather than escalating them to senior management was based on three key operational factors. First, the professional, peer-coaching techniques modeled by my manager, **Matt Douglas**, during my own development sessions equipped me with the necessary strategies to handle the situation effectively at the trainer-trainee level. Second, resolving these issues privately prevented unnecessary embarrassment or professional strain for the associate, which formal management escalation would have introduced. Finally, handling the correction peer-to-peer preserved a constructive trainer-trainee relationship and maintained strong teammate cohesion, facilitating a more receptive learning environment.
