🟦 Peer Training – Santiago Munarriz

Training Period: May 5 – June 5, 2025

Following the successful onboarding of Ben Gordon, I was assigned to train Santiago Munarriz, a new hire joining the logistics team as a home delivery driver. Building upon the original training model I had developed, I refined the structure to make it more immersive, hands-on, and tailored to the unique challenges faced by new drivers.

The revised plan focused on gradually building Santiago’s confidence through controlled practice sessions, real-world dealership scenarios, and clear reinforcement of CarMax’s safety and operational standards.

Training Model Continuity with Improvements

The core training pillars remained the same as those used in previous peer training: truck safety, DOT compliance, pre-trip and post-trip inspections, load securement, use of CarMax apps, and MaxOffer procedures. However, the delivery of the content was enhanced with:

Real-time demonstration: I held dedicated practice sessions at our store where Santiago repeated loading and unloading the same vehicle multiple times over the course of one to two hours. This hands-on repetition ensured muscle memory and confidence in deck positioning, ramp handling, and vehicle alignment.

Visual tools: I provided Santiago with annotated images, diagrams, and checklists to support quicker recall during early field assignments.

Tight-space maneuvering practice: Santiago received specific training on maneuvering and parking the two-car hauler in confined spaces. We practiced during low-traffic hours at the CarMax parking lot, giving him valuable exposure to real-world scenarios in a controlled setting.

Key Training Areas Covered

Safe driving behavior and tail swing awareness in both tight urban locations and highway scenarios

Loading and unloading techniques using proper alignment, with emphasis on large units such as trucks and vans

Deck pinning and hydraulic safety protocols during non-use and transit

Load securement training with Cottrell’s QuiXspinz system, highlighting correct strap positioning and tensioning

MaxOffer pickup procedures, including store 7110 protocols and multi-location dealership releases

Customer interaction, focusing on communication, professionalism, and representing CarMax values on deliveries

Timekeeping and documentation, including the UKG system, CarMax Driver App, and My20

✅ Performance & Engagement

Santiago demonstrated a strong willingness to learn from day one. His focused attitude, proactive approach to hands-on tasks, and thoughtful questions throughout the process made him a standout trainee. He showed consistent growth in both safety procedures and daily operations, and by the end of his training period, he was confidently handling vehicle pickups, inspections, and customer interactions independently.
