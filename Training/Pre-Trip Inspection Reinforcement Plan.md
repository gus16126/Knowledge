
## Pre-Trip Inspection Reinforcement Plan

**(Store 7108 – Phased, Team-Supported Approach)**

### Objective

Reinforce consistent, high-quality pre-trip inspections by:

- Building confidence one driver at a time
- Creating internal peer support
- Minimizing disruption to daily operations
- Establishing repeatable habits, not one-time fixes

---

### Training Structure (Phased & Scalable)

#### Phase 1 – Anchor Driver Training

**Duration:**

- 2 hours per day
- 4 consecutive days
- One driver at a time

**Focus Areas:**

- Full pre-trip inspection flow (start → finish)
- Why each step matters (safety, compliance, equipment protection)
- Common misses observed during prior inspections
- Use of the checklist as a live tool (not memory-based)

**Outcome:**  
Driver completes pre-trip independently, confidently, and consistently.

---
#### Phase 2 – Peer Reinforcement Begins

While **Driver #2** is in training:

- **Driver #1** begins reinforcing the same process with **Driver #3**
- Coaching is observational and supportive, not corrective
- Focus is repetition and standardization

This creates:

- Learning through teaching
- Early culture shift within the team
- Reduced total training time

---
#### Phase 3 – Expand & Sustain

- Repeat the same cycle for remaining drivers
- Drivers trained earlier continue reinforcing standards
- Team ownership increases naturally

---
### Tools Used

- Pre-Trip Inspection Checklist (live reference)
- Short observation notes (what went well / what to reinforce)
- Verbal coaching and repetition
- Team accountability, not correction

---
### Success Indicators

- More consistent inspection flow across drivers
- Fewer missed steps during observation
- Increased confidence during pre-trip walkthroughs
- Improved feedback during future safety observations

---
### Why This Works (Leadership View)

- Put People First: Support instead of criticism
- Win Together: Team reinforces team
- Go for Greatness: Builds lasting habits
- Do the Right Thing: Safety and compliance first
