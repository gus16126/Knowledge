This is Part 4 of a 5-part training series covering key safety and operational behaviors that may be observed during a manager ride-along.

	June 17, 2026

✅ Passing and Lane Changes
• Uses turn signals before lane changes
• Maintains adequate space around the vehicle
• Uses proper passing procedures
• Executes lane changes safely and timely
• Avoids passing on hills and curves
• Maintains awareness of shoulder hazards
• Moves over or slows down for emergency vehicles, construction zones, and pedestrians

✅ Turning
• Accounts for tail swing
• Proper steering control
• Monitors mirrors throughout turns
• Checks blind spots
• Avoids curb strikes
• Maintains 360-degree awareness
• Uses proper turn lanes

Remember: Ride-along are opportunities to demonstrate the safe habits and professional standards we practice every day. Consistency in these areas helps protect our team, our customers, and the vehicles entrusted to us.
