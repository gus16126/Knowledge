### Unsafe Three-Points-of-Contact Practice

In the photo, the driver is exiting the vehicle while holding onto the exterior door frame with the door closed. This places the driver in an unstable position, reducing balance and limiting the ability to maintain proper three points of contact. If the driver's foot slips or loses footing, there is an increased risk of falling from the carrier deck and sustaining injury.

**Correct Procedure:**

When exiting a vehicle on a car hauler, the driver should keep the door open and maintain three points of contact at all times. The driver should use the vehicle's interior grab points, such as the steering wheel, center pillar (B-pillar), or door opening frame, while stepping carefully onto the carrier steps. By keeping the body positioned inside the door opening and using stable handholds, the driver maintains better balance, control, and fall protection throughout the descent.

![[Training-Wrong Three Points of Contact.jpg]]

**Key Safety Reminder:**

Never climb down while hanging from the outside of the vehicle with the door closed. Always use approved access steps and maintain three points of contact using secure interior handholds to ensure a safe and controlled exit.
