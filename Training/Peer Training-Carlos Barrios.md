🟦 Peer Training – Carlos Barrios

Training Period: June 27 – July 11, 2025

Carlos Barrios was the third team member I supported through the home delivery onboarding process. Like Ben Gordon, Carlos followed the original training model I designed to provide new drivers with a step-by-step, hands-on approach to CarMax logistics procedures. The focus remained on reinforcing safe driving practices, operational efficiency, and CarMax core values.

Training Model Consistency

Carlos received the same structured instructional approach that was proven effective during Ben’s training. This included:
A clear progression from observation to guided practice and independent task execution
Daily hands-on instruction in pre-trip and post-trip inspections, vehicle loading, and deck securement
Emphasis on DOT compliance, Hours of Service rules, and BOL documentation
Practice using the CarMax Driver App, My20, and UKG timekeeping systems
Real-world dealership scenarios for MaxOffer pickups and customer deliveries

Key Training Focus Areas
Safe driving behavior, with attention to tail swing, mirror use, and turning radius
Loading alignment techniques, especially for single-load scenarios like vans and trucks
QuiXspinz strap system training, with focus on ratchet alignment and strap wear prevention
Deck pinning procedures for hydraulic safety
MaxOffer pickup procedures at store 7110 and common partner dealerships
Effective and courteous customer interaction during home deliveries

✅ Performance & Engagement
Carlos displayed a proactive attitude and a strong desire to learn from day one. He engaged fully in daily practice sessions, quickly grasped the importance of safety standards, and demonstrated consistent improvement in his confidence and operational skills. By the end of the two-week training period, Carlos was confidently managing pre-trips, load securement, and delivery procedures with minimal assistance.
