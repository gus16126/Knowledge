October 6, 2025

Team,

During a **DOT inspection**, the officer will request to review your **ELD log book records** — the same type of report shown in the example below. This record displays every change in duty status, along with:

- **Start and end times** for each activity (Off Duty, On Duty, Driving, etc.)
- **Locations** and **durations** of each segment
- **Trip notes** such as “Pre-Trip Inspection,” “Deadhead to Lakeland,” and “Post-Trip Inspection”
- **Total miles driven and total duty hours**

This is exactly what the DOT uses to verify that your log is accurate, complete, and compliant with **Hours of Service** rules.

✅ **Key Reminders:**

- Always record each duty change promptly in the ELD.
- Add clear notes for every segment (inspection, meal, unload, etc.).
- Confirm that your log matches your route activity before going off duty.

Keeping clean, accurate records protects you and CarMax during inspections — it reflects our commitment to **Do the Right Thing** and demonstrates our **professionalism on the road**.

Let’s continue to **Win Together** by maintaining precise and compliant logs every day.

Thank you for staying sharp and consistent, team.
Gustavo Guallar
