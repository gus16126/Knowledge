# Class B CDL Training Curriculum Reference
**(Official FMCSA Curriculum Guidelines)**

This document serves as the official, structured training reference outlining the minimum FMCSA entry-level driver training (ELDT) curriculum requirements for Class B Commercial Driver's License (CDL) instruction, based on **Appendix B to Part 380, Title 49**.

## 1. Theory Instruction

### Section B1.1: Basic Operation
*   **Unit B1.1.1: Orientation** 
    *   Introduction to commercial motor vehicle driver training curriculum and vehicle components.
    *   Safety fundamentals and overview of Federal Motor Carrier Safety Regulations (FMCSRs) and hazardous materials (HM) regulations.
    *   Ramifications, fines, and driver disqualification provisions for non-compliance with FMCSR parts 380, 382, 383, and 390–399.
    *   State and local traffic laws, stopping at weigh stations/scales, hazard awareness of vehicle size and weight limitations, low clearance areas (height restrictions), and bridge formulas.
*   **Unit B1.1.2: Control Systems/Dashboard**
    *   Vehicle instruments, gauges, and safety components (belts, mirrors).
    *   Function, location, and proper usage of primary and secondary controls (steering, acceleration, shifting, braking systems [ABS, hydraulic, air], and parking).
*   **Unit B1.1.3: Pre- and Post-Trip Inspections**
    *   Conducting inspections as specified in §§ 392.7 and 396.11, including appropriate inspection locations.
    *   Instruction on enroute vehicle inspections.
*   **Unit B1.1.4: Basic Control**
    *   Basic vehicular control and handling (sharp left and right turns, vehicle centering, maneuvering in restricted areas, and highway entry/exit).
*   **Unit B1.1.5: Shifting/Operating Transmissions**
    *   Shifting patterns and procedures for basic maneuvers, including up and down shifting techniques on multi-speed dual range transmissions.
    *   Importance of fuel economy through proper shifting.
*   **Unit B1.1.6: Backing and Docking**
    *   Safe backing and docking procedures.
    *   "Get Out and Look" (GOAL) guidelines, evaluating facilities, setups, and proper use of spotters.

### Section B1.2: Safe Operating Procedures
*   **Unit B1.2.1: Visual Search**
    *   Visually searching the road for hazards and critical objects (e.g., distracted drivers, pedestrians).
    *   Personal security and general situational awareness at truck stops, rest areas, and shipper/receiver locations.
*   **Unit B1.2.2: Communication**
    *   Communicating intentions to other road users (headlights, turn signals, four-way flashers, horn).
    *   Utilizing eye contact techniques.
*   **Unit B1.2.3: Distracted Driving**
    *   FMCSRs regarding distracted driving, cell phone bans, and texting restrictions (§§ 392.80 and 392.82).
    *   Maintaining visual attention, manual control (hands on wheel), and cognitive focus.
*   **Unit B1.2.4: Speed Management**
    *   Managing speed in response to various road, weather, and traffic conditions.
    *   Calibrating safe following distances under varying weight, length, and weather parameters.
*   **Unit B1.2.5: Space Management**
    *   Managing cushions of space surrounding the vehicle under different traffic and road profiles.
*   **Unit B1.2.6: Night Operation**
    *   Night driving safety, visual changes in darkness, lights management, and speed/space adjustments.
*   **Unit B1.2.7: Extreme Driving Conditions**
    *   Inclement weather operations (cold, hot, rain, wind, fog, snow, ice), steep grades, and sharp curves.
    *   Proper tire chaining procedures.

### Section B1.3: Advanced Operating Practices
*   **Unit B1.3.1: Hazard Perception**
    *   Recognizing hazards to neutralize emergency situations.
    *   Construction and work zone safety protocols.
*   **Unit B1.3.2: Skid Control/Recovery, Jackknifing, and Other Emergencies**
    *   Causes and recovery techniques for skids and jackknifes.
    *   Evasive steering, emergency braking, off-road recovery, and responses to brake failures, tire blowouts, hydroplaning, or rollovers.
*   **Unit B1.3.3: Railroad-Highway Grade Crossings**
    *   Safety regulations, environments, track clearances, obstructed views, signs, signals, and use of Emergency Notification Systems (ENS).

### Section B1.4: Vehicle Systems and Reporting Malfunctions
*   **Unit B1.4.1: Identification and Diagnosis of Malfunctions**
    *   Identifying major vehicle systems (engine, exhaust, brakes, drive train, coupling, suspension).
    *   Understanding component checks, maintenance requirements, and reporting processes.
*   **Unit B1.4.2: Roadside Inspections**
    *   Roadside inspection processes and inspector interactions.
    *   Out-of-Service (OOS) violation criteria, penalties, and operational restrictions (§ 390.5).
*   **Unit B1.4.3: Maintenance**
    *   Basic servicing, fluid checks, preventive maintenance, and simple emergency repairs.

### Section B1.5: Non-Driving Activities
*   **Unit B1.5.1: Handling and Documenting Cargo**
    *   Cargo weight distribution, securement, cargo covering, safe loading/unloading.
    *   Theft prevention and Hazardous Materials (HM) documentation and handling.
*   **Unit B1.5.2: Environmental Compliance Issues**
    *   Recognizing environmental hazards, spills, idling laws, and local/state/federal regulatory restrictions.
*   **Unit B1.5.3: Hours of Service (HOS) Requirements**
    *   Industry-specific HOS requirements, logging daily status (electronic and paper logs), timesheets, and log recaps.
    *   Consequences, fines, and penalties for HOS violations.
*   **Unit B1.5.5: Post-Crash Procedures**
    *   Physical assessment, notifying authorities, protecting the scene, deploying warning triangles, and using fire extinguishers.
    *   Post-crash drug and alcohol testing requirements.
*   **Unit B1.5.6: External Communications**
    *   Interpersonal communication skills for roadside inspections and enforcement officials.
    *   FMCSA English language proficiency requirements.
*   **Unit B1.5.7: Whistleblower/Coercion**
    *   Whistleblower protection regulations (29 CFR part 1978) and reporting coercion to the FMCSA.
*   **Unit B1.5.8: Trip Planning**
    *   Planning routes, fuel stops, safe clearance crossings, size/weight restrictions, and permit procurement.
*   **Unit B1.5.9: Drugs/Alcohol**
    *   Controlled substance rules, prescription drug disclosures, and testing procedures.
*   **Unit B1.5.10: Medical Requirements**
    *   Medical certification rules, examination procedures, and general qualifications (Part 391).

## 2. Behind-the-Wheel Range (BTW Range)
*Trainees must demonstrate proficiency in basic maneuvers using the "Get Out and Look" (GOAL) method:*

*   **Unit B2.1: Vehicle Inspection (Pre-Trip/Enroute/Post-Trip)**
    *   Demonstrating mechanical inspections as specified in §§ 392.7 and 396.11.
*   **Unit B2.2: Straight Line Backing**
    *   Proficiency in straight line backing within appropriate criteria/tolerances.
*   **Unit B2.3: Alley Dock Backing (45/90 Degree)**
    *   Proficiency in alley docking backing configurations.
*   **Unit B2.4: Off-Set Backing**
    *   Proficiency in off-set backing maneuvers.
*   **Unit B2.5: Parallel Parking Blind Side**
    *   Proficiency in blind-side parallel parking.
*   **Unit B2.6: Parallel Parking Sight Side**
    *   Proficiency in sight-side parallel parking.

## 3. Behind-the-Wheel Public Road (BTW Public Road)
*Trainees must demonstrate proficiency in executing public road maneuvers. Units B3.8 through B3.12 require discussion and familiarization but do not require physical demonstration of proficiency:*

*   **Unit B3.1: Vehicle Controls** (Initiating movement, left/right turns, lane changes, curves at highway speeds, interstate entry/exit, controlled stops).
*   **Unit B3.2: Shifting/Transmission** (Performing safe, fuel-efficient shifting).
*   **Unit B3.3: Communications/Signaling** (Intent signaling and roadway communication).
*   **Unit B3.4: Visual Search** (Hazard scanning).
*   **Unit B3.5: Speed and Space Management** (Following distances, traffic adjustment, weight/weather speed calibration).
*   **Unit B3.6: Safe Driver Behavior** (Developing and maintaining defensive driving habits).
*   **Unit B3.7: Hours of Service (HOS) Requirements** (Practical log entry and updates).
*   **Unit B3.8: Hazard Perception** *(Discussion/Familiarization Only)*
*   **Unit B3.9: Railroad (RR)-Highway Grade Crossing** *(Discussion/Familiarization Only)*
*   **Unit B3.10: Night Operation** *(Discussion/Familiarization Only)*
*   **Unit B3.11: Extreme Driving Conditions** *(Discussion/Familiarization Only)*
*   **Unit B3.12: Skid Control/Recovery, Jackknifing, and Emergencies** *(Discussion/Familiarization Only)*
