### What is it?

- The QuiXspinz is a **patented ratcheting system** offered by Cottrell that is used on soft-tie equipment for auto transport trailers.
- The concept: rather than repeatedly inserting and pulling a bar or doing multiple steps to tension a strap, you insert the tie-down bar **once**, then work the ratchet back and forth until the strap is tight.
- It was offered as an upgrade on Cottrell’s “NeXtGen” soft-tie equipment.

---

### Why use it? (Benefits)

- **Efficiency**: The back-and-forth ratchet method means fewer insertion/withdrawal steps, which can save time when loading/unloading vehicles.
- **Operator ergonomics**: The system is designed to simplify and speed up the strap-tightening process, which helps crews move faster and reduce fatigue.
- **Innovation**: The fact that Cottrell updated it (see next section) shows they are committed to “Go For Greatness” and improvement.

---

### Recent Update: QuiXspinz 2.0

- In 2023, Cottrell introduced the **QuiXspinz 2.0** version.
- Key upgrades in 2.0:
    - 100% stainless-steel ratchet cartridge.
    - Powder-coated square shaft for durability and corrosion resistance.
    - Updated cast strap spool.
    - Improved ergonomic grip (better for adverse weather / tough conditions).
- Cottrell states it was phased into production from units built in February/March onwards.

---

### Inspection & Maintenance – What to know

- Even though it’s designed for efficiency, it’s still a mechanical assembly that needs **regular inspection & maintenance**. (“Do The Right Thing.”)
- Some of the inspection guidelines:
    - Check that the ratchet engages in the tightening direction and disengages (free-wheels) in the opposite direction.
    - There should be a **clicking sound** roughly every 20 degrees when rotating in the free-wheeling direction.
    - Check seals around the ratchet hub for looseness or gaps; excess grease around seals should be wiped.
- Maintenance steps:
    - Greasing annually is recommended with a water-resistant grease that protects against rust/corrosion.
    - Seal replacement: remove old seal, install new (PN 137912 according to the bulletin) and re-verify operation.

---

### Things to Watch / Consider (for your use case)

- **Wear & Replacement**: From user forums, some operators mention issues: e.g., dead ratchets, locked up units, or failing to engage properly.
    - > “You pretty much have to replace the entire ratchet box… the ratchet heads actually just bolt onto the tube…”
        
- **Compatibility**: If you have older equipment (pre-2.0) you may want to check if upgrading to the 2.0 is possible or cost-effective for your fleet.
- **Training & Usage**: Since the system operates differently than some standard ratchets, ensure operators are trained on how to identify when it’s working correctly (clicking, engagement/disengagement) so downtime is minimized.
- **Spare Parts / Serviceability**: Because it’s a specialized assembly, keeping spares (seals, ratchet cartridges) and having maintenance procedures clearly defined will save you hassle.
- **Return on Investment**: Consider how much time per strap/tie you’ll save, how many ties per day per operator, and whether the efficiency gain justifies any extra cost (initial or maintenance).

## 1. Spec / Feature Summary for QuiXspinz & QuiXspinz 2.0

Here are what I found — and a few places where info is thin or you’ll want to verify with Cottrell directly.

### Key Features / Updates

- The original QuiXspinz system: patented ratcheting system introduced ~2012 by Cottrell. The ratchet allows the operator to insert the tie-down bar only _once_, then work the ratchet back and forth until strap is taught.
