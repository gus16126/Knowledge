# **Non-Negotiable Safety Rules for Loading and Unloading.**

To support our Associates' safety, we have 15 rules to follow each time a Driver loads or unloads a vehicle. These requirements are intended to help protect our Associates, Vehicles, and Trucks.

    1. Always keep 3 points of contact (no walking up and down the ramp, no using the window as a point of contact).

    2. Whenever possible, secure loads from ground level or the lowest accessible point to avoid climbing on equipment.

    3. Always measure height at the highest point using the height stick.

    4. Decks must always be pinned when they are in use.

    5. The driver must always wear slip-resistant shoes.

    6. Keep hands, limbs, loose articles, jewelry, and clothing clear of moving parts.

    7. Only one person handles loading and unloading the truck, including moving decks, setting pins, securing vehicles, and driving on and off the decks.

    8. To secure the vehicle before the truck moves, confirm loaded vehicles are turned off, windows are up, and doors are locked before operating the truck

    9. There must be at least 6" between the top of the vehicle on the lower deck and the upper deck. Use your flashlight (Part 49xx76) as a guide to measure 6".

    10. No hopping over the center gap or walking side to side on the deck.

    11. Never over-tighten or use excessive force when tightening chains/straps.

    12. Always pull the ratchet or tie-down bar. Never push.

    13. Vehicles must be strapped before operating hydraulics.

    14. A 2 Car hauler cannot move a no runner. A vehicle that requires more than a jump start is a no-runner.

    15. All distractions must be removed while operating on or around the truck and trailer (i.e., no cellphones or headsets). (Keep your cellphone on your person when loading and unloading in case of emergency.)

Driving on the Deck

Driving on the bed of an auto hauler requires slow, intentional movement, precise vehicle placement, and complete focus to prevent accidents and ensure safe transport.

    Distractions - Remove all distractions while operating on or around the truck and trailer (i.e., no cellphones or headsets).

    Adverse Conditions - Use extra caution when wet, oily, or icy conditions are present.

    Vehicle Safety - Prior to loading a vehicle onto a trailer, verify it is in a safe operating condition and has fully functional brakes to prevent accidents.

    Secure floor mats - Remove floor mats or loose items that could interfere with the accelerator.

    Center the Vehicle - Use deck holes to align the vehicle and step out to verify it is centered.

    Operate at a Controlled Speed - Maintain a slow and steady pace while keeping the vehicle centered on the trailer.

    Best Practice Procedures
        Load vehicles using four-wheel drive if the vehicle is equipped.
        Engage the parking brake before exiting.
        Secure vehicle (all windows up) and doors are locked.
        Store all keys in your pockets before exiting the loading vehicle
        Keep your hands free of objects to allow for maximum grip
        Prior to moving a vehicle, HONK to make others aware, especially if you are reversing.

    Monitor Vehicle Placement -When loading vehicles onto the 'trailer/bed' carefully monitor their position to prevent damage to mirrors, doors, tires, and vehicle sides. If necessary, fold mirrors inward to allow for safe clearance.

    Ideal Strap Placement: Keep all straps positioned to the sides of the deck plates. Do not drive over wheel straps or hooks.

    Manual transmission vehicles - Engage the parking brake, and set the vehicle to 1st or reverse gear, depending on the incline.

    Side Rails - Vehicles should be centered on the truck/within the parameters of the securement area for safe operations. Drivers must use ladders and grab bars to safely egress and regress

    Narrow Vehicles - Keep wheels centered per hole guides on deck when loading narrow vehicles (e.g. Smartcar) to prevent slipping or falling through the gap.

    Trailer Support Beams and Clearance - Always watch for trailer support beams, roof clearances, and hinge points.

Prioritize safety by watching your surroundings, inspecting the unit prior to loading, driving slowly, removing distractions, and being cautious in poor conditions.

Plan Load Placement
Planning how you will load the vehicles is a critical component of loading a trailer.

Steps:
1. Identify all the vehicles to be loaded.
2. Arrange the load considering these three factorsWeight: 
    Weigh: Estimate how much each vehicle weighs. You can also refer to the Driver App for the estimated weight.
        - Passenger cars tend to range between 3,200 and 3,600 lbs, while SUVs and trucks can easily exceed 4,500 lb. Electric Vehicles weigh more than gas/hybrid vehicles.

        - Size: Review the height and length of each vehicle.
        - Ensure your load does not exceed the maximum allowable height for your location.
            - Height allowance: 13 6", though some states are 14'.
            - Height may be shorter in some markets. Always review your route and note if you will need to decrease the height of your load based on the road conditions.

        - Arrange the load considering these three factors
    - Route Plan: Understand what vehicles will be unloaded first and plan your load placement in the most efficient order.
    
            - If possible, you only want to move the vehicle twice - once during loading and once during unloading.

3. Below is a typical arrangement:
        - If moving two vehicles:
            - First Vehicle: Small to midsize sedan or SUV, reversed in.
            - Second Vehicle: Midsize to large sedan or SUV.

        - If moving one vehicle:
            - Vehicle: Pull in forward as far as possible to balance axle weights.

* For vehicles that don't start, contact management to follow the No Runner Standard Work.

Loading Steps - Preparing the Truck

1. Engage the PTO Switch
        - Engaging the PTO switch is done with the truck engine at low idle with the parking brakes appplied.
        - If you need to run the cruise control, you can high idle to 1200 rpm by pressing the accel button.

2. Secure the Cab - After exiting the cab, if you have two keys, lock the cab while performing the loading process.

3. Personal Safety- Ensure only one person (primary operator) is on the trailer or operating its equipment (hydraulics) at all times. Any other parties must be on the ground away from the trailer and visible to the operator.
    - Only one person can be operating and loading/unloading the vehicle at one time. Additional parties can get vehicles prepped for loading. But only one person can be responsible for the entire loading/unloading process.

4. Strap Placement- Move all straps away from the driving path to prevent damage and parking on top of them.

5. Prepare the decks - Use your #1 lever to extend the rear ramp all the way out. Walk to the back and flip the flippers/skids onto the ground to load.

Loading Steps
All vehicles are to be loaded with each deck at its lowest point. This will allow the driver to secure the vehicles from the ground and reduce the risk of injury.

Ensure all decks are pinned correctly, even when not in use.

1. Load - Reverse the vehicle onto the trailer into the #1 position, stop and check the center tracking, then continue reversing into position #1.
    -   Strap
2. Approach the #1 Position - Slowly back in until it makes contact with the tire stoppers, positioned on the 8th hole from the cab.
    -   Ensure there is enough room between the car and the cab.
3. Set the Vehicle- Prior to releasing the foot brake, place the vehicle in park and engage the parking brake.
    -   Make sure all windows are rolled up
    -   Turn off the vehicle.Lock the doors
    -   Secure the keys in your pocket until they can be stored in the cab.
4. Secure the Driver's Side from the ground- Start with the front, then continue to the rear, and place a strap around each wheel.
5. Secure the Passenger Side from the ground- Start with the rear, then continue to the front, and place a strap around each wheel.
6. CarMax requires four tie-downs per vehicle.
    -   Do not use the lasso strap/wheel loop strap system (A strap with a loop (lasso) that goes around the tire of the vehicle or the axle strap system (A short strap (axle strap) that wraps around the axle or suspension component) to secure the vehicle.
    -   Place the strap over the wheel. Ensure wheel straps are lying flat (not twisted) and running in a straight line from the ratchet shaft to the tire.
    -   Set the strap hook as close to the tire as possible. By doing so, it will also act as a wheel chock and keep any rocking or vehicle movement to a minimum.The open end of the strap hook should face away from the tire. Wheel straps must be tightened in opposing directions.
    - Ensure tabs of wheel strap cleats are in the center ribs of the tire tread. 
    - Note:
        -   If there is a slow leak/loss of air pressure on a tire, you should not secure that tire with a strap. Three straps are acceptable and still meet DOT requirements.
        -   If the tire is completely flat, the vehicle should not be loaded and should be treated as a no-runner. It can be loaded if a Service Operations Associate installs the spare tire.

Use the guide holes on each deck or ramp plate to ensure the vehicle is centered.
Confirm that there is an equal number of holes visible on both sides of the vehicle to maintain proper alignment

Loading Steps

7. Tighten the straps - Take a two-point stance for stability, pull on the spool/ratchet handle to tighten the binder.
    -   The binder bar should have at least one and a half wraps of strap on the binder spool after tightening for proper securement.
    -   Do not twist the wheel strap during the securement process.
    -   Do not use excessive force or over-tighten wheel straps to secure vehicles.
        -   Excessive force or overtightening means using more force than necessary to secure the vehicle to the equipment properly.
    -   If a tire begins to bulge, you are overtightening the strap. Immediately stop, release the tension, and restart the securement process.

Load the Second Vehicle
1. Set the #2 Deck - Walk to the hydraulic levers, use the #2 hydraulic lever, lift the #1 deck completely to ensure you have the clearance to load the Second Vehicle.
    -   When it is completely raised, place the locking pins on both hydraulic arms and release pressure so the hydraulics rest on the safety pins.
2. Load - Pull in forward into the #2 position, ensuring it is centered on the deck.
    -   Maintain the center by ensuring equal guide holes on both sides of the vehicle.
3. Approach the #2 Position - Slowly pull in until the front tire should land in the solid square between the single flip-up anchor point and the multiple flip-up anchor points when properly parked.
4. Set the Vehicle - Prior to releasing the foot brake, place the vehicle in park and engage the parking brake.
    -   Make sure all windows are rolled up.
    -   Turn off the vehicle.
    -   Secure the keys in your pocket until they can be stored in the cab.
5. Secure the Wheels from the ground - Exit and strap the driver and passenger wheels.

Loading Steps

Preparing the Decks

1. Set the Flippers/Skids - Walk to the back and flip the flippers/skids both in.
2. Slide in Rear Ramp - Use your #1 lever to retract the rear ramp all the way in.
3. Lift the #2 deck- Use the #2 hydraulic lever to take the weight off the safety pins for repositioning.
4. Set Pins - Once both pins are set, bring down the #1 deck slowly until it is resting on the safety pins. Ensure 6" clearance from the bottom of the vehicle to the top deck.

Prep for Drive
1. Measure Height - Using the height stick, measure the highest point of the load on the trailer.
    -   Generally, 13'6", 14' in some states.
    -   Be sure to know height restrictions in the state your vehicle is being operated in and any states it will be driven through to determine the correct height restriction for your load.
2. Final Walkaround- Perform a walkaround, ensuring the following:
        1. All pins are set.
        2. All straps are tight.
        3. All equipment is picked up and put away.
        4. There is clearance around each vehicle that is loaded.
3. Switch Off the PTO

Re-verify straps/chains are secure
Drivers must reverify that the straps/chains are properly tighten, and vehicles secured at whichever of the following comes first:
    1. Every change of duty status
    2. Every 3 hours of driving
    3. Every 150 miles that are drive

Unloading Steps
Unstrap only the vehicles that need to be moved at the stop. Only work on one piece of equipment when loading and unloading.

Unload Second Vehicle
1. Switch On the PTO.
2. Inspect - Walk down the passenger side and check for any road debris.
3. Extend the rear ramps - Use the hydraulic levers to slide the #1 lever out to extend the rear ramps completely.
4. Unfold the extension ramps.
5. Raise the #2 deck - Use the #2 lever to raise the #2 deck completely. Lock both pins on the #2 deck.
6. Unstrap - From the ground, unstrap the passenger's side wheels. Walk around the rear of the deck and unstrap the driver's side wheels on the second vehicle.
7. Unload-While maintaining 3 points of contact, climb the ladder to get into the Second Vehicle and slowly reverse it off the deck. Ensure you keep it centered while unloading.
    -   Before exiting the vehicle, ensure all windows are fully rolled up.
    -   Do not solely rely on the backup camera when unloading because it may cause you to lose the center of the deck.
8. Secure the front wheel straps - for the second vehicle beneath the #1 deck while the pins remain in place.
9. Release the Pins - Return to the levers and raise the #1 deck to release the pins.

Unload the First Vehicle
1. Lower the #1 Deck: - Release both pins and slowly lower the #1 deck, ensuring you are clear of all pinch points.
2. Unstrap the First Vehicle - Unstrap both wheels on the passenger side of the hauler. Walk around to the other side and unstrap both wheels on the driver's side. Return to the other side to get inside the First vehicle.
3. Unload - While maintaining 3 points of contact, climb the ladder to get into the First vehicle and slowly drive it off the deck. Ensure you keep it centered while unloading.
    -   Before exiting the vehicle, ensure all windows are fully rolled up.
4. Secure the Trailer - Flip up both extension ramps and begin securing the straps to the trailer on the passenger side of the hauler. Then, walk to the levers and slide in the #1 lever for the rear deck.

Secure the Trailer
1. Prepare the decks - Use your #1 lever to extend the rear ramp all the way out. Walk to the back and flip the flippers/skids onto the ground to load.
2. Set the Flippers/Skids - Walk to the back and flip the flippers/skids both in.
3. Slide in Rear Ramp - Walk to the hydraulic levers and use the #2 lever to slide in the rear ramp. Lift the #2 deck using the #2 hydraulic lever to take the weight off the safety pins for repositioning.
4. Set Pins - Once both pins are set, bring down the #1 deck slowly until it is resting on the safety pins.

Transporting Electric Vehicles

Driving electric vehicles (EVs) on the bed of a hauler requires special attention to their unique features and safety considerations. The following instructions will guide you through the process, emphasizing the importance of gentle acceleration, seatbelt management, and using transport modes to minimize battery drain.

-   Be Mindful of One-Pedal Driving - Some EVs won't creep forward when your foot is off the brake, and the accelerator pedal is very responsive. Be mindful of one-pedal driving (A driving mode in EVs and hybrids that uses only the accelerator pedal to control both speed and braking) and gentle when applying the accelerator in an EV. Tesla vehicles have this feature enabled by default, and this feature can vary for other makes of EVs.

-   Unbuckle Seatbelts - Ensure EVs have all seatbelts unbuckled before transporting them. CarMax has found leaving a seatbelt buckled on an EV during transport can drain the battery, making it difficult to get the EV off the transporter at its destination.

-   Enable Transport or Shipping Modes - Some EVs (Rivian, Chevrolet EVs, etc.) have dedicated transport or shipping modes to minimize idle battery drain during transport. These can be enabled in the settings screen on the vehicle's infotainment screen, if available. See below image for example of how to enable shipping mode on Rivian vehicles.

        Manufacturer Guides:
        -   Rivian R1T & R1S Tow Operator Guide
        -   Tesla: Instructions for Transporters
        -   Note: Tesla vehicles have a "Transport" mode, but it is only intended for use when winching a vehicle onto or off a hauler and not meant for use when the vehicle is actively being shipped.

Always prioritize being gentle with the accelerator, ensuring seatbelts are unbuckled, and using transport modes to minimize battery drain. If you have any questions or need further assistance, refer to the manufacturer's guides or seek additional training.

Transporting Specialty Vehicles
Specialty vehicles are any vehicles that fall outside the normal size range, including modified vehicles or unmodified larger vehicles. The following instructions will guide you through the process of moving and handling specialty vehicles, emphasizing the importance of extra caution due to their unique clearances and step-in heights.

-   Extra Caution- Use extra caution when moving around and getting in and out of specialty vehicles, as the normal clearances and step-in heights can be drastically different from those of normal vehicles.

-   Loading Tall, Heavy, or Wide Vehicles - If moving especially tall, heavy, or wide vehicles (e.g., cargo vans, dually trucks), they will be the only vehicle loaded onto the truck. When moving a single vehicle, ensure it is fully pulled forward onto the wheel stopper behind the cab.

-   Locating Tires - Locate the front tires as you would a normal pick-up truck and ensure the inside rear wheels are located as normal single rear tires of a pick-up truck would be.

-   Vehicle Weights - Most vehicle weights can be found in the Black Book Invoicing Guides. These are available in the purchasing office or online at https://www.blackbookusa.com.

-   Verifying Height and Clearances - Always verify the height before transportation and know the clearances along your route. Read the clearance signs before crossing under any unfamiliar bridges, underpasses, or other restricted height obstructions.

-   Determining Newer Vehicle Weights - Newer vehicle weights can be determined by using theGVWR weight on the VIN tag and subtracting the cargo and occupant weight found on the TIRE AND LOADING INFORMATION tag located on the driver's door jamb.

Always prioritize using extra caution due to their unique clearances and step-in heights. Ensure that tall, heavy, or wide vehicles are loaded individually and fully pulled forward onto the wheel stopper.
Verify vehicle weights and clearances to avoid any issues during transportation.
