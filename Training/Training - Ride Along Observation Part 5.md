This is Part 5 of a 5-part training series covering key safety and operational behaviors that may be observed during a manager ride-along.

	June 18, 2026

✅ Backing and Parking
• Gets out and checks when needed
• Uses horn to warn others when appropriate
• Activates four-way flashers
• Uses mirrors and cameras effectively
• Maintains slow, controlled backing speed
• Avoids over-steering and under-steering
• Minimizes unnecessary backing

✅ Loading, Unloading, and Securement
• Uses proper lighting (strobes and flashers)
• Verifies overhead clearance
• Chocks wheels when required
• Proper load placement and weight distribution
• Four points of securement per vehicle
• Safe operation of hydraulic systems and pins
• Proper winch operation
• Double-checks load securement before departure
• Safely loads and unloads vehicles

Remember: Ride-along are opportunities to demonstrate the safe habits and professional standards we practice every day. Consistency in these areas helps protect our team, our customers, and the vehicles entrusted to us.
