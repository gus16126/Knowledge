📘 **Florida Commercial Driver’s License Manual**  
**Section 3.2.3 – Don’t Be Top-heavy**

> _The height of the vehicle's center of gravity is very important for safe handling. A high center of gravity (cargo piled up high or heavy cargo on top) means you are more likely to tip over. It is most dangerous in curves, or if you have to swerve to avoid a hazard. It is very important to distribute the cargo so it is as low as possible. Put the heaviest parts of the cargo under the lightest parts._

This authoritative guidance confirms the same safety concepts we apply daily when loading and positioning vehicles.

Let’s continue using these principles to keep our loads stable, our trucks safe, and our operations consistent.
