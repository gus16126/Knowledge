### Two-Car Hauler Loading Configuration – Sedans

The image above shows a two-car hauler loaded with two sedan vehicles. In this example, **both vehicles are loaded front first**, and the **locking pins are positioned at approximately 11½ holes**.

Loading two sedans **front first** can provide several safety and operational advantages.

First, loading the **upper deck vehicle front first** improves weight distribution and overall vehicle stability. The engine compartment is typically the heaviest part of a sedan, and positioning that weight toward the front of the carrier helps balance the load more effectively.

Second, loading sedans **in reverse can increase the risk of front bumper contact with the ground**. When a sedan is backed up the ramp, the rear of the vehicle rises while the front of the vehicle moves downward toward the ground. Because many sedans have a lower front bumper and longer front overhang, this movement can bring the bottom of the front bumper closer to the ground, increasing the risk of scraping.

By loading the vehicle **front first**, this risk is reduced because the front of the vehicle is moving upward along the ramp rather than downward toward the ground.

For these reasons, when conditions allow, loading sedans **front first on both positions** can improve:

- Vehicle stability on the carrier
- Load balance
- Protection of the vehicle’s front bumper

This approach supports safer loading practices and helps protect both the equipment and the vehicles being transported.
