# **CarMax Promotion Readiness Packet**

## **MAP · IDP · LDP Evidence Integration**

### **Authoritative Promotion Decision Framework**

---

## **Purpose of This Document**

This document defines the **Promotion Readiness Packet** as the formal, integrated record used to evaluate and support leadership promotion decisions at CarMax.

It ensures that promotion decisions are:

- Evidence-based
- Behavior-driven
- Consistent across leaders and locations
- Aligned to CarMax Values and business priorities

The Promotion Readiness Packet does not create readiness—it **confirms it**.

---

## **Role of the Promotion Readiness Packet**

Within the CarMax Leadership Pipeline:

- **MAP** assesses readiness
- **IDP** develops capability
- **LDP** validates execution
- **Promotion Readiness Packet** integrates evidence to support a decision

This packet provides a **complete, defensible view** of leadership readiness.

---

## **What the Promotion Readiness Packet Is (and Is Not)**

### **The Packet Is**

- A consolidated leadership record
- A decision-support tool
- A values-aligned evaluation artifact
- A calibration-ready summary

### **The Packet Is Not**

- A nomination form
- A justification narrative
- A performance review
- A guarantee of promotion

It exists to ensure **fairness, clarity, and consistency**.

---

## **Required Packet Components**

A complete Promotion Readiness Packet contains **five required sections**:

---

### **1. MAP Summary & Readiness Assessment**

**Purpose**  
To document assessed leadership readiness and identified development needs.

**Includes**

- MAP readiness level (Ready Now / Developing / Not Yet Ready)
- Key strengths demonstrated
- Identified development gaps
- Summary of evaluator observations

This section establishes the **baseline leadership assessment**.

---

### **2. IDP Summary & Development Outcomes**

**Purpose**  
To confirm that MAP-identified gaps were addressed through intentional development.

**Includes**

- IDP focus areas
- Development goals
- Actions taken
- Evidence of behavioral change
- Leader feedback and validation

This section confirms that development was **effective and sustainable**.

---

### **3. LDP Execution & Validation**

**Purpose**  
To validate real-world leadership execution at the next-level expectation.

**Includes**

- Acting assignments completed
- Scope of responsibility
- Observed leadership behaviors
- Outcomes achieved
- Feedback from leaders and peers

This section provides **proof of applied leadership capability**.

---

### **4. Integrated Leadership Evidence**

**Purpose**  
To consolidate leadership behaviors across all pipeline phases.

**Evidence Categories**

- People leadership and coaching
- Ownership and accountability
- Decision-making and judgment
- Communication and influence
- Operational excellence and safety leadership
- Values-based leadership

Evidence must demonstrate **consistency across time and context**.

---

### **5. Promotion Readiness Determination**

**Purpose**  
To formally document leadership alignment and readiness for next-level role consideration.

**Includes**

- Overall readiness assessment
- Identified risks or watch-outs (if any)
- Leader alignment statement
- Recommendation status

This section enables **clear, confident decision-making**.

---

## **Readiness Standards for Promotion Consideration**

A candidate is considered promotion-ready when:

- Leadership behaviors are demonstrated consistently
- Development gaps have been effectively closed
- Acting leadership execution confirms independence
- Evidence aligns across MAP, IDP, and LDP
- Values-based leadership is evident under pressure

Promotion readiness is defined by **reliability**, not potential.

---

## **Risk Identification & Mitigation**

The packet also supports responsible promotion decisions by identifying:

- Remaining development risks
- Role-specific readiness considerations
- Support needs post-promotion

This reflects proven leadership best practices: **strong promotions reduce downstream performance risk**.

---

## **Calibration & Review Use**

Promotion Readiness Packets are intended to support:

- Talent calibration discussions
- Succession planning
- Leadership reviews
- Promotion decision forums

Consistent packet use promotes **fairness and equity** across the organization.

---

## **Roles & Accountability**

### **Candidate**

- Owns development and execution
- Demonstrates readiness through behavior

### **Leader**

- Validates evidence objectively
- Provides coaching and assessment
- Ensures alignment to CarMax standards

### **Organization**

- Applies consistent promotion criteria
- Supports leaders post-promotion

---

## **What Strengthens a Promotion Case**

- Long-term consistency
- Multi-source evidence
- Clear leadership impact
- Alignment between assessment, development, and execution
- Strong values alignment

---

## **What Weakens a Promotion Case**

- Inconsistent behaviors
- Incomplete development follow-through
- Overreliance on technical performance
- Limited real-world leadership exposure
- Unresolved readiness risks

---

## **Promotion Integrity**

The Promotion Readiness Packet exists to protect:

- The candidate
- The team
- The leader
- The business

Promoting the right leaders, at the right time, **the right way** is how CarMax wins together.
