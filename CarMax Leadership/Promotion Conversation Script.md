# **CarMax Promotion Conversation Script**

## **Clear · Respectful · Values-Aligned**

### **Leader Guide for Promotion & Development Conversations**

---

## **Purpose of This Script**

This script provides leaders with **consistent, values-aligned language** for promotion-related conversations.

It ensures that conversations are:

- Clear and respectful
- Evidence-based
- Supportive of development
- Protective of promotion integrity

This script is not meant to be read verbatim.  
It is a **guide for tone, structure, and intent**.

---

## **When to Use This Script**

Leaders should use this framework when:

- Communicating promotion decisions
- Discussing readiness outcomes
- Providing development-focused feedback
- Maintaining trust and transparency

---

## **Conversation Structure (All Scenarios)**

Every promotion conversation should include:

1. Purpose and respect
2. Evidence-based discussion
3. Clear outcome
4. Forward-looking support

---

## **Scenario 1 — Promotion Approved**

### **Opening**

> “I want to take time to recognize the leadership you’ve been consistently demonstrating. This conversation is about your readiness and next step.”

### **Evidence-Based Feedback**

> “Over time, you’ve shown strong ownership, sound decision-making, and consistent people leadership. Your MAP results, IDP progress, and LDP execution all align with next-level expectations.”

### **Decision**

> “Based on that evidence, we’re moving forward with your promotion.”

### **Values Alignment**

> “How you’ve led—especially under pressure—reflects CarMax values, and that matters.”

### **Next Steps**

> “Promotion is a new starting point. We’ll continue supporting your development as you transition into this role.”

---

## **Scenario 2 — Promotion Deferred (Developing)**

### **Opening**

> “I appreciate the leadership you’re demonstrating, and I want to be clear and supportive about where things stand.”

### **Evidence-Based Feedback**

> “You’re showing progress in several leadership areas, and we’ve seen improvement. At the same time, we need more consistency in a few key behaviors before moving forward.”

### **Decision**

> “At this point, promotion is not the next step—but continued development is.”

### **Clarity Without Discouragement**

> “This isn’t a setback. It’s about making sure when you do step into the role, you’re set up to succeed.”

### **Forward Plan**

> “We’ll focus your IDP on these areas and continue building readiness together.”

---

## **Scenario 3 — Not Yet Ready**

### **Opening**

> “I want to have an honest and respectful conversation about leadership readiness.”

### **Evidence-Based Feedback**

> “Right now, we’re not seeing consistent leadership behaviors at the next-level expectation. This is based on observable patterns, not a single moment.”

### **Decision**

> “Because of that, promotion isn’t appropriate at this time.”

### **Supportive Reframe**

> “This doesn’t define your potential. It gives us clarity on where to focus development.”

### **Development Commitment**

> “If leadership growth is something you want, we’ll work together to build the skills and behaviors needed.”

---

## **Scenario 4 — Associate Asks About Promotion Timing**

### **Response**

> “Promotion isn’t tied to a timeline—it’s tied to readiness. When leadership behaviors are consistent and validated, promotion conversations move forward.”

---

## **Key Language Guidelines**

Leaders should:

- Use “we observed” instead of “I feel”
- Reference behaviors, not traits
- Avoid promises or timelines
- Reinforce values
- Keep the focus on growth and readiness

---

## **What to Avoid**

❌ “You’re almost there” without evidence  
❌ “Just keep doing what you’re doing”  
❌ Comparing to others  
❌ Making implied guarantees  
❌ Avoiding clarity

Clarity builds trust—even when the answer is difficult.

---

## **Leader Self-Check Before the Conversation**

Ask yourself:

- Do I have evidence?
- Am I being clear?
- Am I aligned to CarMax values?
- Am I supporting development responsibly?
- Am I protecting promotion integrity?

---

## **Closing Statement (All Scenarios)**

> “My role is to support your growth and be honest about readiness. We’ll keep working together the right way.”
