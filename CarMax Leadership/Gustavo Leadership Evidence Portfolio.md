**🟦 Training Development – Pre-Trip Inspection Step-by-Step Checklist**

**Date Range: June 20, 2025 – August 5, 2025**

Between June 20 and August 5, 2025, I developed a detailed, step-by-step Pre-Trip Inspection Checklist based on the official CarMax Pre-Trip Inspection Job Aid.

CarMax’s published job aid outlines the required components to be inspected on every truck and establishes a general inspection flow—top to bottom and left to right—ensuring consistency across drivers and locations. While the job aid clearly defines _what_ must be checked and the intended inspection order, I identified an opportunity to further support team members by translating that guidance into a more granular, step-by-step inspection process.

Using the job aid as a source of requirements, I created a structured checklist that guides drivers through each inspection section in sequence, directing them component by component from the beginning of the inspection to the end. The checklist was designed to eliminate guesswork, reduce missed steps, and support consistency—especially for newer drivers or those still building confidence with under-hood and chassis inspections.

The intent of this checklist was not to replace the CarMax job aid, but to **operationalize it**—turning high-level inspection expectations into an organized, repeatable process that reinforces standardization, accuracy, and accountability during daily pre-trip inspections.

This tool supports safer operations, stronger compliance, and reinforces the importance of completing inspections thoroughly and in the correct order every time.

📌 **Why It’s Important to Complete a Pre/Post-Trip Inspection with Each Use**

The Pre/Post-Trip Inspection is a **critical safety procedure** that must be completed **every time** a vehicle is used. This process has been **established to standardize** how inspections are performed across the team, ensuring that every driver is aligned with CarMax's expectations for safety, readiness, and operational consistency.

Completing this inspection helps ensure:

- **Standardization**: Everyone follows the same method, reducing errors and building consistency across all shifts and drivers.
    
- **Vehicle Safety**: Mechanical issues, fluid leaks, or equipment defects can be identified before they cause breakdowns or safety hazards.
    
- **Vehicle Readiness**: Ensures that the vehicle is properly equipped and prepared for use, reducing downtime and surprises on the road.
    
- **Efficiency**: Addressing issues before departure prevents delays, rework, and potential incidents during delivery operations.
    

As a team, it's essential that we **not only perform these inspections**, but also understand _why_ they matter. This reinforces our commitment to safety, professionalism, and _Doing the Right Thing_ every day.
