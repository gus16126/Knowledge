POLICY 
Leave & Accommodation Resources 
PURPOSE / SCOPE  
The purpose of this policy is to provide an overview of the leave and accommodation processes to ensure that 
Associates understand the processes for applying for leaves of absence and workplace accommodations. 
For full guidance on leave and accommodation options, including specific eligibility criteria, timelines, and 
additional resources, Associates should refer to the detailed policies available once logged in to the Benefits 
Website - benefits.carmax.com and/or CarMax World.

Continuous Leave: Allows for time off for an extended, uninterrupted period. For example, an Associate needs 
to miss work for three weeks due to a medical procedure. 
Intermittent Leave: Allows for time off in separate blocks rather than all at once. This means instead of taking 
an extended period of leave, time off may be granted as needed, either by: 
• Taking days off periodically (e.g., missing one day per month for medical condition flare-ups) 
• Shorter absences throughout the day (e.g., missing .5 hours, per day to attend medical treatments)

Workplace Job Accommodations 
Job accommodations include, but are not limited to ergonomic, workplace or schedule modifications, policy 
exceptions, job restructuring, or job reassignment, or additional leave. 

When to Apply 
1. If you are requesting an accommodation at work due to a chronic medical condition that is impacting 
your ability to perform the essential functions or core duties/responsibilities of your job. 

2. If you are requesting an accommodation due to religious beliefs or practices. 
How to Apply 
✓ You must initiate the claim by completing a Request for Accommodation via the online portal , which can 
be accessed at work or from home.

Absent without Leave 
Associates are required to follow the proper procedures when requesting and certifying a leave of absence. 
"Absent without leave" occurs when an Associate fails to provide the required documentation to support their 
leave request or does not comply with leave policies. 

This includes: 
1. Failing to request a leave of absence within 14 calendar days of the first day of absence. 

2. Not submitting required documents, such as medical certificates or legal notices, by the specified 
deadlines.

3. Remaining absent after approved leave is exhausted or denied. 
Absences without leave that are not otherwise covered by approved sick, personal or other protected time 
may result in disciplinary action, up to and including separation of employment.
