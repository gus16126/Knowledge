At CarMax, S.M.A.R.T. is a behavioral and performance framework used for setting goals and evaluating results. It stands for:

S – Specific

Goals should be clear, well-defined, and unambiguous.
Example: “Increase customer satisfaction survey scores by 10%.”

M – Measurable

You should be able to track progress and measure the outcome.
Example: “Sell at least 15 vehicles this month.”

A – Achievable (or Attainable)

The goal should be realistic and within your capability or resources.
Example: “Learn to use a new software system within 2 weeks.”

R – Relevant

The goal should align with team or company objectives and your role.
Example: “Improve vehicle reconditioning turnaround to support sales goals.”

T – Time-bound

Every goal should have a deadline to maintain urgency and focus.
Example: “Complete all ADP training modules by the end of the quarter.”

⸻

In CarMax’s culture, associates are often encouraged to use SMART goals during:
	•	Performance reviews
	•	Development planning (especially in the ADP program)
	•	Project planning
	•	Team goal-setting and one-on-ones with managers
