# **BEST SPECIFIC EXAMPLE** 

During my time on the fleet team, I identified a significant safety concern involving the condition and operation of the rollback winches. My experience with winch operations and observations of damaged cables revealed an unsafe procedure in use, posing a risk to both the team and the public. 

# **Planning & Execution** 

I took the time to pause and assess the issue thoroughly. I documented the unsafe conditions with photos and gathered evidence to understand the scope of the problem. Recognizing the urgency of the situation, I prioritized addressing the issue immediately and broke it down into manageable, bite-sized steps to address both the immediate issue and long-term prevention: 

1. Immediate Action: I recommended taking the winches out of service until they could be repaired. 



2. Training Program: I proposed creating a winch operation training program to educate team members on proper usage. 3. Follow-Up and Sustainment: I suggested regular inspections of the winch for signs of improper use to ensure the winches remained in safe working condition. 

I presented these steps to my lead driver in a clear, organized manner, emphasizing the importance of each action and how they would collectively resolve the issue. I explained how the training program would improve safety and operational standards while the follow-up inspections would sustain these improvements over time. 

As a result of my recommendations, the winch cables were repaired, and steps were taken to address the safety issues, including the development of a training program. This proactive approach not only resolved the immediate safety concern but also created a sustainable system to prevent future issues. By breaking the problem into bite-sized solutions and organizing a plan for follow-up and sustainment, I demonstrated my ability to identify problems, assess risks, prioritize effectively, and offer sound recommendations to improve operations and safety.
