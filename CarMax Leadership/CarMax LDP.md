# **CarMax Leadership Development Pathway (LDP)**

## **Acting Assignments · Execution · Readiness Validation**

### **Authoritative Leadership Application Framework**

---

## **Purpose of This Document**

This document defines the **Leadership Development Pathway (LDP)** as the execution and validation phase of the CarMax Leadership Pipeline.

The LDP confirms that leadership capability demonstrated during MAP and developed through the IDP can be **applied consistently in real operating environments**.

The LDP answers one critical question:

> **Can this leader perform at the next level—consistently, independently, and aligned to CarMax Values?**

---

## **Role of the LDP in the Leadership Pipeline**

Within the integrated pipeline:

- **MAP** evaluates leadership readiness
- **IDP** develops targeted capability
- **LDP** validates sustained execution

The LDP is not preparation—it is **proof**.

---

## **What the LDP Is (and Is Not)**

### **The LDP Is**

- Application-based
- Evidence-driven
- Behavior-focused
- Conducted in live operations
- Supported through coaching, not supervision

### **The LDP Is Not**

- A training course
- A trial promotion
- A checklist of tasks
- A short-term performance spike

The LDP measures **how leaders operate when responsibility is real**.

---

## **Core LDP Components**

The LDP consists of three required components:

1. **Acting Leadership Assignments**
2. **Expanded Ownership & Influence**
3. **Readiness Validation**

---

## **1. Acting Leadership Assignments**

### **Purpose**

Acting assignments place candidates in situations where next-level leadership behaviors are required, not simulated.

### **Characteristics of Strong Acting Assignments**

- Time-bound but meaningful
- Visible to leaders and peers
- Require independent judgment
- Involve people leadership and decision-making
- Carry real operational impact

### **Examples**

- Leading a team or function during leader absence
- Coordinating operational execution across groups
- Facilitating training or process reinforcement
- Owning safety, quality, or performance initiatives
- Mentoring or onboarding associates

Assignments should **stretch capability without overwhelming support**, reflecting best-practice leadership development principles.

---

## **2. Expanded Ownership & Influence**

### **What Evaluators Look For**

- Ownership beyond assigned tasks
- Influence without positional authority
- Decision-making aligned to CarMax Values
- Accountability for outcomes

### **What Candidates Demonstrate**

- Proactive leadership
- Clear communication and expectation-setting
- Coaching and feedback behaviors
- Follow-through and consistency

Leadership impact must extend **beyond personal performance**.

---

## **3. Readiness Validation**

### **How Readiness Is Validated**

Readiness is confirmed through:

- Sustained execution over time
- Multiple leadership scenarios
- Consistent feedback from leaders and peers
- Demonstrated independence

Validation focuses on **repeatable leadership behavior**, not isolated success.

---

## **Leadership Behavior Validation Areas**

LDP validation aligns to the same core domains evaluated in MAP:

- People leadership and coaching
- Ownership and accountability
- Decision-making and judgment
- Communication and influence
- Operational excellence and safety leadership
- Values-based leadership

This ensures continuity and fairness across the pipeline.

---

## **Evidence Expectations During LDP**

Acceptable LDP evidence includes:

- Observed leadership behaviors
- Feedback from associates and leaders
- Measurable improvements in outcomes
- Consistency across multiple situations
- Reduced need for guidance or correction

Evidence must demonstrate **sustainability**.

---

## **Support & Coaching During LDP**

### **Leader Role**

- Observe without over-directing
- Coach in real time
- Provide timely, specific feedback
- Validate readiness objectively

### **Candidate Role**

- Seek feedback proactively
- Reflect and adjust behaviors
- Own outcomes fully
- Demonstrate learning agility

Effective LDP execution reflects a **coach-supported, candidate-owned** model, aligned with proven leadership development practice.

---

## **What Strong LDP Performance Looks Like**

- Independent leadership execution
- Confidence without overreach
- Consistent values-aligned decisions
- Positive influence on others
- Operational stability and improvement

Strong performance shows **readiness to step into role without disruption**.

---

## **What Weakens LDP Credibility**

- Reliance on constant direction
- Inconsistent leadership behaviors
- Avoidance of difficult conversations
- Short-term performance spikes
- Lack of reflection or adjustment

The LDP reveals gaps that MAP and IDP alone cannot.

---

## **LDP Completion Criteria**

An LDP is considered complete when:

- Leadership behaviors are demonstrated consistently
- Evidence confirms readiness at next level
- Leader and candidate alignment is clear
- Minimal developmental risk remains

Completion signals **promotion readiness**, not guaranteed promotion.
