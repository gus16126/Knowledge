# **CarMax Leader Coaching Playbook**

## **Developing, Assessing, and Sponsoring Talent**

### **Authoritative Guide for Leaders Supporting MAP, IDP, and LDP**

---

## **Purpose of This Document**

This document defines the **Leader Coaching Playbook** for CarMax leaders who support associates through the Leadership Pipeline.

It establishes a consistent, values-aligned approach for leaders to:

- Coach leadership behaviors effectively
- Assess readiness objectively
- Sponsor talent responsibly
- Protect promotion integrity

This playbook ensures leaders develop talent **the CarMax way**—with clarity, fairness, and accountability.

---

## **Leader Role in the Leadership Pipeline**

Leaders are the **primary enablers** of leadership readiness.

Within the pipeline:

- **MAP** requires objective observation
- **IDP** requires active coaching
- **LDP** requires supported execution
- **Promotion** requires responsible sponsorship

Leaders do not create readiness alone—but readiness **cannot exist without strong leadership coaching**.

---

## **Core Coaching Principles**

Effective leadership coaching at CarMax is grounded in the following principles:

1. **Behavior Over Intent**  
    Coach what is observed, not what is promised.
2. **Evidence Over Opinion**  
    Anchor feedback in facts, patterns, and outcomes.
3. **Consistency Over Intensity**  
    Reinforce steady leadership behaviors over time.
4. **Support Without Dependency**  
    Enable independence, not reliance.
5. **Values First, Always**  
    Align coaching to CarMax Values in every conversation.

These principles reflect proven leadership development practices adapted to CarMax’s operating culture.

---

## **Coaching Across the Pipeline**

### **1. Coaching During MAP**

**Leader Responsibilities**

- Observe leadership behaviors consistently
- Separate performance from leadership capability
- Provide clear, timely feedback
- Avoid pre-judging outcomes

**Effective MAP Coaching Behaviors**

- Share specific observations
- Clarify expectations
- Reinforce strengths
- Identify development gaps without ambiguity

MAP coaching builds **awareness**, not defensiveness.

---

### **2. Coaching During IDP Execution**

**Leader Responsibilities**

- Help translate feedback into behaviors
- Create real development opportunities
- Observe progress actively
- Adjust development actions as needed

**Effective IDP Coaching Behaviors**

- Regular check-ins
- Behavior-focused feedback
- Reinforcement of progress
- Honest discussion of gaps

Leaders coach **growth**, not compliance.

---

### **3. Coaching During LDP Execution**

**Leader Responsibilities**

- Provide stretch opportunities
- Observe leadership under real pressure
- Coach without over-directing
- Validate readiness objectively

**Effective LDP Coaching Behaviors**

- Allow decision-making autonomy
- Intervene only when necessary
- Provide real-time feedback
- Encourage reflection and learning

LDP coaching confirms **sustainability**, not short-term success.

---

## **Feedback Quality Standards**

High-quality leadership feedback at CarMax is:

- **Specific** – Anchored in observable behavior
- **Timely** – Delivered close to the event
- **Balanced** – Reinforces strengths and gaps
- **Actionable** – Clearly tied to next steps

Vague feedback slows development and weakens readiness.

---

## **Sponsorship vs. Advocacy**

### **What Sponsorship Means**

- Validating readiness with evidence
- Supporting visibility and opportunity
- Standing behind leadership capability

### **What Sponsorship Is Not**

- Favoritism
- Personal advocacy without evidence
- Pressure to promote prematurely

Strong sponsorship protects both the candidate **and** the business.

---

## **Calibration Discipline**

Leaders play a critical role in maintaining promotion fairness through calibration.

**Calibration Expectations**

- Use shared language and standards
- Compare evidence, not narratives
- Challenge assumptions respectfully
- Align on readiness thresholds

Calibration ensures **equity, consistency, and credibility**.

---

## **Common Coaching Pitfalls to Avoid**

- Coaching tasks instead of behaviors
- Over-protecting candidates from challenge
- Delaying difficult feedback
- Relying on potential instead of proof
- Sponsoring without sufficient evidence

Avoiding these pitfalls preserves pipeline integrity.

---

## **What Strong Leader Coaching Looks Like**

- Clear expectations
- Honest feedback
- Consistent observation
- Thoughtful development opportunities
- Responsible sponsorship decisions

Strong leaders develop other leaders by **modeling leadership themselves**.

---

## **Leader Self-Accountability**

Leaders should regularly reflect:

- Am I observing consistently?
- Am I coaching behaviors or outcomes only?
- Am I enabling independence?
- Am I upholding CarMax Values?
- Am I protecting promotion integrity?

Leadership development starts with **leader discipline**.
