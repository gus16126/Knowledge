# **CarMax Individual Development Plan (IDP) Framework**

## **Goals · Evidence · Tracking · Accountability**

### **Authoritative Leadership Development Guide**

---

## **Purpose of This Document**

This document defines the **CarMax Individual Development Plan (IDP)** as a formal leadership development tool that converts MAP feedback into **measurable, behavior-based growth**.

It establishes:

- How IDPs are structured
- What strong development goals look like
- How progress is tracked and validated
- How leaders and candidates share accountability

The IDP is not a formality. It is the **bridge between assessment and readiness**.

---

## **Role of the IDP in the Leadership Pipeline**

Within the CarMax Leadership Pipeline:

- **MAP** identifies readiness gaps
- **IDP** closes those gaps intentionally
- **LDP** validates sustained readiness

The IDP ensures leadership growth is:

- Structured
- Observable
- Supported
- Accountable

---

## **Core Principles of an Effective IDP**

An effective IDP at CarMax is:

1. **Behavior-Based**  
    Focuses on what leaders do, not what they intend.
2. **Evidence-Driven**  
    Progress is validated through observable actions and outcomes.
3. **Role-Aligned**  
    Development aligns to next-level leadership expectations.
4. **Time-Bound**  
    Progress is tracked within defined timeframes.
5. **Shared Accountability**  
    Candidates own execution; leaders own coaching and feedback.

(These principles reflect proven leadership development best practices adapted to CarMax’s operating model.)

---

## **IDP Structure**

Each IDP is built around **four required components**:

### **1. Development Focus Area**

A specific leadership behavior or capability identified through MAP feedback.

**Examples**

- Coaching effectiveness
- Decision-making under pressure
- Ownership and follow-through
- Cross-functional influence
- Safety leadership

---

### **2. Development Goal (Behavioral Outcome)**

Goals must define **observable leadership behavior**, not tasks.

**Strong Goal Characteristics**

- Clearly describes the behavior change
- Tied to CarMax Values
- Relevant to next-level role expectations

**Example**

> “Demonstrate consistent coaching behaviors by conducting regular development conversations that result in observable improvement in associate performance.”

---

### **3. Development Actions**

Actions define **how** the behavior will be practiced in real work.

**Effective Actions**

- Stretch assignments
- Acting leadership responsibilities
- Coaching opportunities
- Peer mentoring
- Training facilitation

Actions must occur in **real operating environments**, not simulations only.

---

### **4. Evidence & Measurement**

Evidence validates progress and prevents subjectivity.

**Acceptable Evidence**

- Observable behavior change
- Feedback from leaders or peers
- Measurable improvements in outcomes
- Consistent execution over time

Evidence must show **repeatability**, not one-time success.

---

## **IDP Goal Quality Standards**

To ensure development effectiveness, CarMax IDP goals should meet the following internal quality standards (adapted from widely accepted leadership development research):

- Clear and specific
- Focused on behaviors
- Challenging but achievable
- Directly tied to role expectations
- Measurable through observation or outcomes

Vague goals weaken development and delay readiness.

---

## **Progress Tracking & Review Cadence**

### **Tracking Expectations**

- Progress is reviewed regularly, not at the end
- Evidence is documented and discussed
- Adjustments are made as needed

### **Recommended Review Cadence**

- **Monthly:** Progress check-ins
- **Quarterly:** Formal IDP review
- **Ongoing:** Real-time coaching and feedback

This cadence reflects best practices for sustaining behavior change while remaining operationally realistic.

---

## **Roles & Accountability**

### **Candidate Accountability**

- Owns execution of development actions
- Seeks feedback proactively
- Demonstrates learning agility
- Applies feedback consistently

### **Leader Accountability**

- Provides coaching and observation
- Creates development opportunities
- Gives timely, honest feedback
- Validates evidence objectively

Leadership development is a **partnership**, not a handoff.

---

## **What Strong IDP Progress Looks Like**

- Increased leadership confidence
- More consistent decision-making
- Improved influence and communication
- Observable impact on others
- Reduced need for direction

Progress is judged by **behavioral consistency**, not effort alone.

---

## **Common IDP Pitfalls to Avoid**

- Overloading too many goals
- Focusing on tasks instead of behaviors
- Infrequent feedback
- Lack of evidence
- Passive ownership

Avoiding these pitfalls accelerates readiness and credibility.

---

## **IDP Completion Criteria**

An IDP is considered complete when:

- Target behaviors are demonstrated consistently
- Evidence confirms sustainability
- Leader and candidate alignment is clear
- MAP readiness gaps are effectively closed

Completion does not end development—it signals **readiness to progress**.
