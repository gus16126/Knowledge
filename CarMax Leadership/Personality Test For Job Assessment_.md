Which of the following describes best a manager?
I recognize weak arguments. I give thought to the opinions of others.
My argument is always right.
The statement that best describes a manager is: 
      "I give thought to the opinion of others."
Effective managers need to be open to feedback, consider diverse perspectives, and collaborate with their team to make informed decisions. Recognizing weak arguments is also a valuable skill, but a manager's ability to listen, empathize, and incorporate the opinions of others is crucial for building trust, fostering teamwork, and achieving success.

               “Delegation.”
The most important trait for a manager can vary depending on the context, but Delegation is often considered the most critical. Here's why:

What does this mean? 
“I often bear the brunt of decisions that you don’t fully agree with”.
This means that someone frequently has to deal with the negative consequences or responsibilities of decisions they did not fully support or agree with. In a managerial or professional context, this could imply:
Having to implement decisions made by higher-ups or others, even if you personally disagree with them.
Facing challenges or backlash from those decisions, such as team dissatisfaction, operational difficulties, or unmet goals.
Taking responsibility for outcomes, even if you weren't fully on board with the decision-making process.
This is a common experience for managers, as they often have to balance their own opinions with the need to follow organizational directives or consensus-driven choices. It highlights the importance of adaptability, professionalism, and the ability to navigate difficult situations while maintaining team morale and productivity.
