# IDP Strategy – What I Control

> The internal completion criteria for CarMax's IDP Management Program are not fully transparent. That's fine. This note focuses on what I do control: building an undeniable record, managing upward visibility, and ensuring the IDP is valuable regardless of which door opens.

**Last Updated:** July 4, 2026

---

## The Portable Record

If I had to describe my IDP experience to a hiring manager at another company, here's what reads as management-ready:

| Evidence | Weight | Status |
|---|---|---|
| **Peer training program** — Designed and ran structured onboarding for 6 associates | Heavy | ✅ Documented |
| **Training resource development** — Interactive apps, audio briefs, ride-along guides, videos | Heavy | ✅ Documented |
| **Safety leadership** — Pre-trip standardization, team communications, real-world hazard examples | Heavy | ✅ Documented |
| **Values-based coaching** — Applied Code of Conduct, Five Dysfunctions framework with associates | Medium | ✅ Documented |
| **Operational problem-solving** — Dealership workflow optimization, modified vehicle recovery | Medium | ✅ Documented |
| **Leadership studies** — Five Dysfunctions of a Team applied to daily coaching | Light | ✅ Documented |
| **Quantified impact** — Inspection pass rates, app adoption, training outcomes | Heavy | 🔴 Missing |
| **Management-facing proposals** — Written recommendations submitted upward | Medium | 🔴 Missing |
| **Formal peer feedback collection** — Testimonials from trained associates | Light | 🔴 Missing |

### Priority: Quantify impact

Every piece of evidence is stronger with numbers. Next time I create a resource or train an associate, capture:
- How many people used it?
- What improved? (Inspection consistency? Error reduction? Time saved?)
- What did management observe?

## Stakeholder Playbook

### Matthew Douglas — Immediate Manager, IDP Sponsor

**What he sees:** Daily operations, coaching moments, training sessions, pre-trip observations, team communications. He's the closest witness to my work.

**What he cares about:** Team performance, safety compliance, associate development, operational consistency.

**How to keep him in the loop:**
- Monthly reporting is already happening — continue
- Flag when a training resource ships (app, video, guide) so he knows it exists
- Ask directly: "What would you like to see more of from me?"

**Key question to ask Matt at the right moment:**
> "When you think about what makes someone ready for the next level, what's the one thing you'd want to see from me that you haven't yet?"

### Jessy Pesantes — Matt's Manager, Strategic Decision-Maker

**What he sees:** Reports from Matt, aggregate team performance, promotion recommendations. He doesn't see my daily work — he sees Matt's summary of it.

**What he cares about:** Leadership pipeline health, team readiness, culture carriers.

**How to reach him (through Matt):**
- My visibility to Jessy depends entirely on what Matt communicates upward
- Matt needs clean, concise material to pass along — not raw data, but impact summaries
- The Role Guide Assessment is the formal channel; informal upward mentions come from Matt choosing to share something

**Key question (through Matt, at the right moment):**
> "Is there anything from my development you think Jessy should be aware of at this stage?"

## Evidence Gap Scan

Based on what a hiring manager or promotion panel would look for — regardless of company:

| Gap | Why it matters | How to fill it |
|---|---|---|
| **Quantified outcomes** | "Trained 6 associates" is good. "6 associates, 100% passed road tests on first attempt" is undeniable | Track pass rates, inspection scores, onboarding duration |
| **Upward proposals** | Shows I can think at the manager level, not just the peer level | Write one safety or process recommendation to Matt in memo format |
| **Peer feedback** | Third-party validation is stronger than self-reporting | Ask trained associates for informal feedback; capture quotes |
| **Cross-team visibility** | Regional Lead means oversight beyond one store | Volunteer for cross-store support, APU coverage at other locations |
| **P&L or resource awareness** | Management roles involve budgets, equipment, scheduling | Learn how fleet maintenance costs, fuel, and overtime are tracked |

### Immediate next action

Pick one gap and close it this month. The highest-impact, lowest-effort one is **quantified outcomes** — just start tracking one number per training activity.

---

## Monthly Pulse Check

At the end of each month, answer three questions:

1. **What did I build or ship?** (New resource, training session, process improvement)
2. **Who saw it?** (Matt? The team? Did it reach Jessy?)
3. **What's the number?** (How many people? What improved? What was the result?)
