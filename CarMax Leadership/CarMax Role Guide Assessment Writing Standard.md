# CarMax Leadership Assessment Methodology

### Internal Writing & Evaluation Instructions

**Version:** 2.0  
**Purpose:** Standard methodology for preparing competency-based Role Guide Assessments and leadership documentation.  
**Primary Audience:** Associates, mentors, managers, and leadership candidates.

---

# Purpose

This assessment is **competency-driven, not accomplishment-driven.**

When completing the CarMax **Role Guide Assessment Tool (Lead / IC to Manager)**, the objective is **not** to summarize accomplishments or reproduce a self-evaluation.

The objective is to demonstrate, through specific evidence, that the associate consistently exhibits the leadership behaviors defined by each Role Guide competency, with emphasis on the evaluation level being assessed.

This methodology evaluates leadership behaviors through the lens of **CarMax's Leadership Philosophy**, ensuring that every competency reflects not only operational excellence but also alignment with CarMax's Core Values, Leadership Expectations, and organizational culture.

Accordingly, all examples, evidence, and supporting narratives should naturally reinforce CarMax's leadership philosophy whenever applicable:

* **Win Together** – Build strong teams, collaborate effectively, and help others succeed.
* **Put People First** – Invest in associate development, support customers, and foster professional growth.
* **Go For Greatness** – Demonstrate continuous improvement, innovation, and operational excellence.
* **Do The Right Thing** – Act with integrity, accountability, professionalism, and respect.

CarMax's leadership vocabulary should be used naturally to strengthen the connection between demonstrated leadership behaviors and the company's cultural expectations. The vocabulary should reinforce the evidence—not replace it.

---

# Core Principles

- Competency-driven, not accomplishment-driven.
- Behavior-driven, not task-driven.
- Recurring evidence outweighs isolated accomplishments.
- Leadership influence outweighs individual achievement.
- The competency definition determines the story—not the accomplishment.

---

# Evaluation Level Determination (First Step)

Before analyzing any competency or writing a single sentence, determine which performance level the document is intended to evaluate.

* **Current Level:** Demonstrate consistent execution of the responsibilities and behaviors expected in the associate's current role.

* **Next Level:** Demonstrate recurring leadership behaviors already being performed that align with the expectations of the next role, regardless of formal title or authority.

**This decision determines:**

* The evidence selected.
* The writing style.
* The examples emphasized.
* The interpretation of accomplishments throughout the assessment.

**Never begin writing until the evaluation level has been clearly established.**

---

# Primary Rule

Do not write about what the associate accomplished.

**Write about the competency behavior the accomplishment demonstrates.**

Every statement should directly answer the competency definition.

---

# Evidence Hierarchy

## Current Level

When documenting Current Level performance, prioritize evidence that demonstrates:

1. Consistent execution
2. Reliability
3. Quality of work
4. Teamwork and collaboration
5. Operational results

---

## Next Level

When documenting Next Level readiness, prioritize evidence that demonstrates:

1. Recurring leadership behaviors
2. Associate development and coaching
3. Process improvements with lasting impact
4. Organizational influence
5. Operational accomplishments that demonstrate leadership

Operational accomplishments should only be used when they clearly demonstrate leadership behaviors.

---

# Competency Alignment Process

For every competency:

## Step 1

Read the official competency definition.

Ask:

> **What leadership behavior is CarMax actually measuring?**

Do not begin writing until the behavior being evaluated is clearly understood.

---

## Step 2

Review the leadership portfolio and identify the strongest evidence demonstrating that behavior.

Prefer:

* Multiple occurrences
* Different associates
* Sustained behaviors
* Repeatable leadership actions

Avoid isolated accomplishments unless they clearly demonstrate the competency.

---

## Step 3

Structure the example around the competency—not around the project.

The competency determines the story.

The accomplishment provides the evidence.

---

# Evaluation Writing Strategy

## Current Level Assessment

The objective is to demonstrate that the associate consistently performs the expectations of the current role.

Select evidence that best matches the competency definition before selecting the strongest accomplishment.

Focus on:

* Consistent execution of responsibilities
* Reliable day-to-day performance
* Collaboration
* Professionalism
* Sound judgment
* Operational consistency

When writing:

* Describe what the associate consistently does.
* Emphasize execution rather than leadership.
* Demonstrate reliability through recurring performance.
* Avoid overstating leadership behaviors.
* Support statements with recurring evidence.

Ask yourself:

> **Does this example demonstrate consistent performance at the associate's current role?**

---

## Next Level Assessment

The objective is to demonstrate that the associate is already performing the leadership behaviors expected of the next role.

Select evidence that best matches the competency definition before selecting the strongest accomplishment.

Focus on:

* Coaching
* Associate development
* Leadership influence
* Decision making
* Process improvement
* Accountability
* Organizational impact

When writing:

* Describe leadership behaviors rather than operational accomplishments.
* Emphasize initiative rather than participation.
* Demonstrate recurring leadership.
* Show influence beyond individual performance.
* Connect every accomplishment directly to the competency definition.

Ask yourself:

> **Does this example demonstrate readiness for the next role?**

---

# Calibration Questions

Before finalizing every competency ask:

## Recurrence vs. Occurrence

Was this behavior demonstrated repeatedly?

If only once, search for stronger recurring evidence.

---

## Initiated vs. Instructed

Did the associate recognize the opportunity?

Did they take initiative?

Or were they simply following instructions?

Prefer proactive leadership.

---

## Role Scope vs. Role Readiness

Does this example demonstrate:

* Excellent execution within the current role?

or

* Leadership behaviors expected of the next role?

Use this distinction to ensure the evidence matches the intended evaluation.

---

## Validated vs. Viewpoint

Can this behavior be validated through:

* Manager observations
* Associate development
* Team communications
* Training resources
* Operational improvements
* Measurable outcomes

Use observable evidence whenever possible.

---

# Writing Rules

Every competency should answer the definition directly.

Avoid listing accomplishments.

Instead demonstrate:

* Leadership behavior
* Decision making
* Coaching
* Accountability
* Influence
* Operational impact

---

# Leadership Behaviors by Competency

## Values

Protects and preserves organizational culture.

Demonstrate how the associate:

* Champions CarMax Values
* Protects and preserves CarMax culture
* Reinforces professionalism
* Coaches ethical behavior
* Models company expectations

Do not simply mention the four values.

Demonstrate them.

---

## Developing Talent

Focus on:

* Daily coaching
* Observations
* Timely feedback
* Performance trends
* Associate development

Training resources support the competency.

They are not the competency.

---

## Planning & Execution

Demonstrate:

* Problem identification
* Breaking complex problems into practical solutions
* Structured planning
* Follow-up
* Sustainment

Show systems, not isolated fixes.

---

## Working Through Others

Do not focus on formal authority.

Instead demonstrate:

* Assigning responsibility
* Coaching
* Following up
* Holding others accountable
* Developing independence

The competency measures leadership behavior—not job title.

---

## Directions of Influence

Demonstrate:

* Building trust
* Gaining buy-in
* Influencing peers
* Influencing leadership
* Communicating operational improvements

Whenever possible, include examples of upward influence.

---

## Leadership Style

Demonstrate:

* Leading by example
* Reinforcing positive behaviors
* Recognizing initiative
* Inspiring ownership
* Creating a positive learning environment

Leadership Style reflects how others experience the associate's leadership.

---

# Preferred Evidence

Use evidence demonstrating:

* Multiple associates developed
* Recurring coaching
* Standardized training
* Sustainable improvements
* Leadership influence
* Team development
* Operational consistency

---

# Common Writing Mistakes

Avoid:

* Describing accomplishments instead of behaviors.
* Listing tasks instead of demonstrating leadership.
* Using isolated examples when recurring evidence exists.
* Forgetting to answer the competency definition.
* Selecting evidence that does not match the evaluation level.
* Confusing excellent execution with leadership readiness.
* Apologizing for lacking formal authority.
* Writing the same accomplishment under multiple competencies without emphasizing the different competency behavior being demonstrated.

---

# Final Calibration Check

Before finalizing every competency ask:

> **If the competency title were removed, could a calibration panel correctly identify the leadership behavior being demonstrated?**

If the answer is **No**,

rewrite the example.

---

# Desired Outcome

Every completed competency should allow a calibration panel to conclude:

> **"This associate consistently demonstrates the behaviors expected of the evaluated level through recurring actions, measurable influence, associate development, operational excellence, and alignment with CarMax values."**

The final document should read as evidence of leadership behavior—not as a summary of work performed.

# Methodology Workflow

Every competency evaluation should follow the same decision sequence:

1. Determine the evaluation level (Current or Next).
2. Read the competency definition.
3. Identify the leadership behavior being measured.
4. Select the strongest recurring evidence.
5. Verify that the evidence matches the competency definition.
6. Align the narrative with CarMax's Leadership Philosophy.
7. Draft the competency statement.
8. Perform the Final Calibration Check before submission.

Never reverse this sequence.

The competency definition determines the evidence—not the other way around.

---

# Methodology Maintenance

This methodology is intended to be a living document.

After each completed Role Guide Assessment, Self-Evaluation, IDP, MAP, LDP, or promotion cycle, review this methodology and update it with lessons learned, stronger examples, improved writing practices, and new leadership insights.

Document improvements should focus on:

- Better alignment with competency definitions.
- Stronger evidence selection.
- Improved calibration techniques.
- More effective leadership storytelling.
- Better integration with CarMax's Leadership Philosophy and Vocabulary.

The objective is continuous refinement so that each future assessment is more accurate, more competency-focused, and more reflective of demonstrated leadership behaviors than the previous version.
