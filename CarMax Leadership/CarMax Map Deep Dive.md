# **CarMax MAP Deep Dive**

## **Leadership Behaviors & Evidence**

### **Evaluator & Candidate Reference**

---

## **Purpose of This Document**

This document provides a detailed, behavior-based view of what the **Management Assessment Program (MAP)** evaluates and how leadership readiness is demonstrated at CarMax.

It is intentionally designed for **dual use**:

- **Evaluators** use it to assess leadership readiness consistently and objectively
- **Candidates** use it to understand expectations and demonstrate readiness intentionally

This document reinforces that MAP is an **evidence-driven assessment**, grounded in observable leadership behaviors aligned to CarMax Values.

---

## **How MAP Is Evaluated**

MAP evaluates **how leaders think, act, and influence**, not just what they know.

Readiness is determined by:

- Consistency of behaviors over time
- Decision quality in real operating environments
- Impact on people, safety, and results
- Alignment to CarMax Values under pressure

MAP assessments are based on **what is demonstrated**, not what is stated.

---

## **Core Leadership Behavior Domains**

MAP evaluates leadership across the following integrated domains:

---

### **1. People Leadership & Coaching**

**What Evaluators Look For**

- Coaches associates toward improvement, not compliance
- Provides timely, constructive feedback
- Builds trust and psychological safety
- Develops others beyond immediate task execution

**What Candidates Demonstrate**

- Regular coaching conversations
- Ownership of associate development
- Willingness to have difficult but respectful conversations
- Recognition of effort and improvement

**Evidence Examples**

- Coaching plans or follow-ups
- Development conversations
- Observable behavior change in others

---

### **2. Ownership & Accountability**

**What Evaluators Look For**

- Takes responsibility without deflection
- Owns outcomes, both positive and negative
- Follows through consistently
- Anticipates issues before escalation

**What Candidates Demonstrate**

- Proactive problem-solving
- Closure on open items
- Accountability language (“I own this”)
- Reliable execution without reminders

**Evidence Examples**

- Resolved operational gaps
- Self-identified improvement actions
- Follow-through documentation

---

### **3. Decision-Making & Judgment**

**What Evaluators Look For**

- Makes sound decisions with incomplete information
- Balances people, safety, and business needs
- Escalates appropriately
- Learns from outcomes

**What Candidates Demonstrate**

- Calm decision-making under pressure
- Use of data and policy together
- Reflection and adjustment after outcomes

**Evidence Examples**

- Decisions made during operational challenges
- Risk-aware choices
- Post-decision learning conversations

---

### **4. Communication & Influence**

**What Evaluators Look For**

- Clear, respectful communication
- Ability to influence without authority
- Consistency of message across audiences
- Active listening

**What Candidates Demonstrate**

- Clear expectations
- Adaptable communication style
- Effective cross-functional collaboration

**Evidence Examples**

- Meeting facilitation
- Alignment conversations
- Peer influence without positional authority

---

### **5. Operational Excellence & Safety Leadership**

**What Evaluators Look For**

- Models safety-first behaviors
- Reinforces standards consistently
- Identifies risk early
- Leads by example

**What Candidates Demonstrate**

- Visible safety advocacy
- Process adherence
- Coaching others on standards

**Evidence Examples**

- Safety observations
- Process improvements
- Risk mitigation actions

---

### **6. Values-Based Leadership**

**What Evaluators Look For**

- Decisions aligned to CarMax Values
- Ethical consistency
- Respect for people and process
- Courage to do the right thing

**What Candidates Demonstrate**

- Values-driven decisions
- Integrity under pressure
- Fair and respectful treatment of others

**Evidence Examples**

- Ethical decisions
- Advocacy for people
- Consistent values language in actions

---

## **Readiness Levels (How MAP Interprets Evidence)**

### **Ready Now**

- Demonstrates all core behaviors consistently
- Operates independently at next-level expectations
- Positively impacts people and results
- Requires minimal support

### **Developing**

- Demonstrates many behaviors, not yet consistent
- Needs targeted development
- Shows learning agility and coachability

### **Not Yet Ready**

- Inconsistent leadership behaviors
- Requires foundational development
- Limited evidence of sustained leadership impact

MAP focuses on **patterns**, not isolated moments.

---

## **What Strengthens a MAP Outcome**

- Long-term consistency
- Clear evidence across multiple domains
- Alignment between words and actions
- Observable impact on others
- Willingness to learn and adjust

---

## **What Weakens a MAP Outcome**

- One-time performance spikes
- Over-reliance on technical skill
- Defensiveness to feedback
- Inconsistent values application
- Ownership gaps

---

## **Evaluator & Candidate Alignment**

MAP is most effective when:

- Expectations are transparent
- Feedback is continuous
- Development is intentional
- Evidence is visible and documented

This alignment ensures fairness, clarity, and readiness integrity.
