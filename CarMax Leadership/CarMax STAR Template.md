The STAR Template

The STAR Template is commonly used at CarMax and many other companies for:

Performance reviews
Leadership programs (IDP, LDP, ADP)
Interview questions
Promotion discussions
Documenting accomplishments
Behavioral examples for competencies such as Teamwork, Communication, Leadership, and Safety

Instead of simply saying "I trained a new associate," STAR helps you tell the complete story and demonstrate impact.

Example Using Your CarMax Training Experience

S – Situation

The logistics team hired a new Home Delivery Driver, Leonardo, who was unfamiliar with the equipment, toolbox inventory, and two-car hauler requirements. Truck 232557's toolbox was missing several required items and needed to be reorganized.

T – Task

As an experienced driver and peer trainer, I needed to help ensure the truck was properly equipped, support Leonardo's onboarding, and reinforce equipment readiness standards for the team.

A – Action

I worked with Leonardo to review the required toolbox inventory and truck equipment standards. Leonardo cleaned and reorganized the toolbox, replaced the missing spill kit, added two spare straps with ratchets, and replaced all tie-down straps on the upper and lower decks. I documented the improvements with before-and-after photos and shared a training message with the team to reinforce the importance of maintaining organized and properly stocked toolboxes.

R – Result

Truck 232557 was returned to a fully operational and organized condition, improving equipment readiness and supporting safe operations. The training communication also increased awareness of toolbox standards and encouraged team ownership of shared equipment.

Reflection

This experience reinforced the value of coaching, communication, and leading by example. By recognizing positive behaviors and turning them into a team learning opportunity, I helped support a culture of accountability, teamwork, and operational excellence—skills that are important for future leadership roles.

For your CarMax IDP (Individual Development Plan) and future leadership opportunities, STAR is especially powerful because it transforms:

"I posted a toolbox message."

into

"I identified an opportunity to improve equipment readiness, coached a new associate, documented improvements, shared a training communication with the team, and reinforced operational standards."

That's the difference between describing an activity and demonstrating leadership impact.

# **Example:**

🟦 **Self-Evaluation Entry**

 - **Toolbox Organization & Equipment Readiness Initiative (June 10, 2026):** Identified and addressed an equipment readiness opportunity on Truck 232557 by coaching a new associate through the organization and replenishment of required toolbox supplies. Documented before-and-after improvements, recognized positive initiative, and converted the experience into a team training resource that reinforced safety, equipment accountability, and operational excellence. This effort supported associate development while helping establish consistent equipment readiness standards for the team.

The self-evaluation entry demonstrates several CarMax leadership competencies simultaneously:

Teamwork – partnered with a new associate.
Put People First – coached and developed Leonardo.
Communication – created and shared a training resource.
Safety & Operational Excellence – reinforced equipment accountability and readiness.
Leadership Influence – turned a local improvement into a team-wide learning opportunity.
Go For Greatness – improved a process instead of simply accepting the current condition.

For a self-evaluation, reviewers are usually less interested in the toolbox itself and more interested in the behaviors behind the accomplishment.
