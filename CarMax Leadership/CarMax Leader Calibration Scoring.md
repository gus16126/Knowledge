# **CarMax Leader Calibration Scoring Guide**

## **Consistent Evaluation · Fair Decisions · Promotion Integrity**

### **Authoritative Calibration Reference**

---

## **Purpose of This Guide**

This guide provides leaders with a **shared scoring and calibration framework** to support consistent leadership assessments and promotion decisions at CarMax.

It ensures that:

- Leaders evaluate leadership behaviors using the same standards
- Evidence is weighted consistently
- Calibration discussions are objective and fair
- Promotion integrity is protected across teams and locations

This guide supports—not replaces—leader judgment by **anchoring decisions in evidence**.

---

## **When to Use This Guide**

Leaders should use this guide during:

- MAP evaluations
- IDP progress reviews
- LDP validation
- Promotion readiness discussions
- Talent calibration sessions

Calibration is most effective **before** decisions are finalized.

---

## **Core Calibration Principles**

Effective calibration at CarMax is grounded in:

1. **Behavior Over Potential**  
    What is consistently demonstrated matters more than what might be possible.
2. **Evidence Over Narrative**  
    Decisions rely on observable facts, not persuasive storytelling.
3. **Patterns Over Moments**  
    Repeated behaviors carry more weight than isolated events.
4. **Consistency Over Comparison**  
    Leaders are evaluated against standards—not against each other.
5. **Values Alignment Always**  
    How results are achieved matters as much as the results themselves.

---

## **Leadership Behavior Domains**

Calibration evaluates leadership across six standardized domains:

1. People Leadership & Coaching
2. Ownership & Accountability
3. Decision-Making & Judgment
4. Communication & Influence
5. Operational Excellence & Safety Leadership
6. Values-Based Leadership

These domains align directly to MAP, IDP, and LDP expectations.

---

## **Scoring Scale (Standardized)**

Each domain is scored using the following scale:

### **4 – Consistently Exceeds**

- Demonstrates next-level leadership behaviors independently
- Positively influences others
- Serves as a role model
- Requires minimal guidance

### **3 – Meets Expectations**

- Demonstrates expected leadership behaviors consistently
- Handles responsibility reliably
- Responds well to feedback

### **2 – Developing**

- Demonstrates some leadership behaviors
- Inconsistency remains
- Requires ongoing coaching and support

### **1 – Not Demonstrated**

- Leadership behaviors are limited or absent
- Requires foundational development
- Readiness gaps are significant

Scores must be supported by **specific evidence**.

---

## **Evidence Weighting Guidelines**

Leaders should consider evidence from:

- Direct observation
- Feedback from peers and associates
- Outcomes from acting assignments
- IDP progress and results
- Values-based decisions under pressure

Heavier weight should be given to:

- Recent evidence
- Multi-source validation
- Real-world execution

---

## **Calibration Discussion Framework**

During calibration sessions, leaders should ask:

- What behaviors have we consistently observed?
- What evidence supports this score?
- Where have behaviors held under pressure?
- Are we seeing independence or reliance?
- Are values demonstrated when it matters most?

Calibration conversations should be **fact-based and respectful**.

---

## **Scoring Interpretation**

### **Promotion-Ready Profile**

- Majority of domains scored **3 or 4**
- No critical domain scored **1**
- Evidence supports consistency and independence

### **Developing Profile**

- Mix of **2s and 3s**
- Clear development plan in place
- Progress visible but not yet sustained

### **Not Yet Ready Profile**

- Multiple **1s or low 2s**
- Gaps in core leadership behaviors
- Insufficient evidence of readiness

---

## **Common Calibration Risks to Avoid**

- Overweighting technical performance
- Being influenced by tenure or likability
- Ignoring values-based concerns
- Relying on future potential instead of current evidence
- Avoiding difficult alignment conversations

Avoiding these risks strengthens trust in the system.

---

## **Leader Accountability in Calibration**

Leaders are expected to:

- Arrive prepared with evidence
- Use shared language and standards
- Challenge respectfully
- Support fair outcomes

Calibration credibility depends on **leader discipline**.

---

## **Governance & Use Standards**

- This guide should be used consistently
- Scores should be documented
- Outliers should be discussed, not ignored
- Adjustments should reflect evidence, not pressure
