        Vehicle Delivery Driver, Mr. Gustavo Guallar, did not adhere to the CarMax Fleet Associate Guide - Driver Handbook, which states that Fleet Associates have the responsibility of safeguarding CarMax property from loss, damage, and theft. This responsibility includes their Fleet equipment and all vehicles being transported.  Mr. Guallar did not demonstrate consistent behaviors in the following CarMax Associate 1 Competencies. Analysis and Decision Making: Uses all available resources to make good decisions; thinks logically; has a thought process that is practical and grounded; takes time necessary to make the right choice. Results Focus: Gets the work done well, on time, following the right process; displays dependability and reliability; makes and follows through on commitments.
        Question
        Question

        BACKGROUND: (e.g. behaviors observed, dates and times)
    Manager
        Answer
        Answer

        On February 20, 2026, Vehicle Delivery Driver, Mr. Gustavo Guallar, arrived at 6141-RPB to collect an assigned customer transfer, 28301853, 2024 Toyota RAV4.  The vehicle was to be delivered to the 7113-BOY location.  He partnered with an Associate at 6141 and offered to help pull the car but was told that help was not required and he could wait in the loading area.  Mr. Guallar proceeded to wait in his truck.  Once the vehicle was taken to the loading area by the Inventory Associate, Mr. Guallar compared the vehicle information in his Driver App with the stock number listed on the transfer bag.  He did not, however, verify the stock and vin number on the vehicle.  The result was that Mr. Guallar loaded and delivered a 2023 Toyota Crolla Cross (28256528) to 7113-BOY instead.

         

        Field Logistics Manager, Matt Douglas, conducted a discovery conversation with Mr. Gustavo Guallar on Wednesday, February 25, 2026.  During this conversation, Mr. Guallar was presented with the route and RecovR information that indicated an incorrect vehicle had been delivered.  Mr. Guallar took ownership of the mistake and acknowledged he had forgotten to check the stock and vin numbers on the car.
